East Subcommittee to the Committee for the Design of the Southwest Bicycle and Pedestrian Commuter Path (SWP)

Proposed Design Recommendations, 9 June 1998

  1. Introduction

    2. This document represents the work product of the East Subcommittee to the Committee for the Design of the Southwest Bicycle and Pedestrian Commuter Path, a formally constituted committee of the City of Madison. This document is divided into two parts. The first part is a normative section that represents the committees design goals and recommendations. The second part is a rationale section that documents concerns raised during the process and how they are addressed.

    1.1 About this Document

    This document was created to facilitate discussion and communication toward the goal of furthering the goals of the committee. It is intended that this or a similar document be formally adopted as the committee's working document.

    1.2 Vision Statement

    The trail and corridor should safely meet the needs of bicycle commuters, recreational bikers, walkers, skiers, persons with disabilities and dog owners and should be aesthetically pleasing to users and neighbors. The corridor should be designed and operated to minimize and alleviate undesirable impacts, preserve compatible uses and be an asset to adjacent land owners and neighbors.

    1.3 Terminology

    This document refers to the proposed Southwest Bicycle and Pedestrian Commuter Path as the path. The Committee for the Design of the Southwest Bicycle Pedestrian Commuter Path is referred to as the SWP Committee. [ed- other terms?]

  2. Goals

The design recommendations that follow shall be consistent with the following specific goals. The goals express overall design attributes and shall be consistent with the Vision Statement.

2.1 Specific Goals

  1. Provide a safe environment to all users of the path. Users of the path include but are not limited to cyclists, pedestrians, pedestrians in wheelchairs, and roller-bladers.
  2. Provide amble space on the path to reduce conflicts between the various users of the path.
  3. Provide a safe and direct route for all users of the path to and from destinations.
  4. Provide access to the path such that destinations would reasonably include but not be limited to work, school, the store, bus stops, the UW Arboretum, West Mooreland Park, UW Hospital, UW campus, Monroe Street shops, Cherokee School, Edgewood HS and college and the neighbors house.
  5. Provide a secure environment for those living adjacent to the path and to those using the path.
  6. Provide landscaping so as to improve the appearance of the path and reduce mosquito breeding areas.

  1. Recommendations

Design recommendations of the committee are intended to provide guidance to city engineers in the development of a design for the construction of the path. Aspects of design that are not specified should be determined by city engineers in a manner that is consistent with the Goals specified in this document.

  1. The path should be a ten to twelve foot paved path with cleared shoulders.
  2. The path should be a minimum width of ten feet. It should be twelve feet where possible without lowering the grade more than one foot.
  3. Dranage areas should be constructed in areas where water is likely to collect and stand. These dranage areas should stand off from the path far enough so that they do not pose any hazard to users of the path.
  4. Pedestrian crossing islands should be installed in Midvale and Odana roads.
  5. Traffic lights that stop traffic on Midvale at the path crossing.
  6. Wiring conduits for lighting should be installed when the path is built. This installation of lighting on the eastern segment should be the considered at a later time.
  7. Access should be provide at the following points but not limited to these locations:
    • Chippawa Drive
    • Anthony Lane
    • Gregory St. (near Glenwood)
    • Virigina Terrace
    • Sheldon St.
    • Prospect St.
    • Oakland Ave.
