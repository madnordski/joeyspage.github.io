Joe's rain rules:

  1. If the forecast calls for scattered rain and/or thundershowers, RIDE. In Wisconsin, forecasters tend to forecast scattered rain and thundershowers whenever they don't have a clue (which is most of time).

  2. If it looks like rain when you're starting out but isn't raining, RIDE. I've had many wonderful (and dry) rides by following this rule. I can remember days when the air was so calm it was scary and other rides under the most dramatic skies you've ever seen.

  3. If it's drizzling and this is your best chance for a long ride this week, RIDE. No exemptions for having just washed and waxed the bike.

  4. If it's flat out raining and you have to drive 20 minutes or more to the start of the ride, GO. By the time you get there it may stop. I love riding right after a rain, the air is intoxicating. Besides, if you carry your bike outside of the car and you don't ride, you can just towel it off when you get back and your bike is cleaned.

  5. If it's flat out raining when you're ready to get on the bike, RIDE. Exemptions include any old excuse you can think of, just don't let it be the rain.

  6. If it's spitting lightning out of the ski just as you put foot to pedal, RIDE. Ride back to the car, home, or nearest shelter, that is.
