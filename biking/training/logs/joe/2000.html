by Joseph King and Scott Rose

27 February Out Seminole to the newly created Capitol City trail. Then through Fitchburg to Lacy road, Seminole, and through the Arb. It was sunny and windy. I was lucky to finish with a 13.9 average. 18.4 18.4
4 March The ride to Paoli was sunny and the road was filled with cyclists. One group of about fifty invited me to join them but I passed. 27.9 46.3
25 March Pat and I did her weekday morning ride just to start things off for her. This takes us out Seminole Hwy. to Whalen and back. Today it included a side trip to Pasqual's. 15.2 61.5
26 March My Riley loop: Owen - Tokay - Charles - Odana Golf Course - Bike Path/Bridge - Hammersley - Elver Park - Midtown - Timber - Paulson - Cty J - Mauer - Timber - Old Sauk - Twin Valley - Demming Way - Greenway - High Point - Elmwood - (Middleton Pasqual's) - Old Middleton - Rosa - Marconi - Knutson - Regent - Meadow Lane It was a lovely day, warm enough for a short selves, with just a few drops of rain. It would appear that Midtown road will be lost to cyclists a few years ahead of schedule. 28.3 89.8
1 April Our first training ride as a group. We had Jack, Dan, Evan, Adam, and Barbara. We thought we were also going to have rain but it never showed up. 47.9 137.7
2 April Just a short and easy ride out Seminole to Grandview. I returned to Seminole on the new Capital City Trail. One could also see that the ties were dug up for what will be the Badger Trail. Unlike the Capital City trail, the Badger Trail will be gravel and it will run all the way to Freeport, Illinois. 16.2 153.9
5 April WNR from Paoli. Dennis, Dan, and I did the ride together. It was windy but nice. 20 173.9
12 April It was a cool day which might explain my usually fast 17.7 average speed. I have lots of fine memories on this ride. Tim Waddington reminded me of how, as ride leader, I got him lost even as snow flakes were falling on this ride two years ago. Tonight's ride was boring actually as I spent a big part of it in pace lines. Oh that might explain the higher the usual average speed as well. 25.5 199.4
15 April New Glarus Training Ride It was a perfect day for a ride. A bit cool at times but very pleasant as was the company. Lunch in New Glarus in the spot. I had a portabella burger w/fries. Dan had dutch apple pie making this an official pie ride. The ride from Dan's to my home was the toughest bit for me. The wind and cold seemed to increase just as I hit Timber Lane from Mauer. 60.8 260.2
22 April Our RTR group heads to Lodi. Leaving from Festge Park near Cross Plains we find clear skies and crisp air. Although the temperature was hovering near 50, the bright sun soon had us storing our jackets and tights. The wind-assisted trip up Lodi-Springfield road was a joy. The trip back required more effort but it afforded some picture postcard views. Besides the Great Blue Heron, Sand Hill Crane, and Mallards we were treated to a flock of American Coots floating on Crystal Lake. Just down the road I spotted a small collection of Wood Ducks paddling in the calm waters of a small pond. On my return to Madison I took Airport Road which is a newly paved two lane road in good biking condition from Cross Plains to just east of Capital View Road. After that, I turned off onto Demming Way which quickly reached a dead end. I pressed on through the gravel and sand surface to find a bike path the runs behind the Bruce Company. Unfortunately, it is a bike path to now where and I was a muddy mess before I made it out to Donna Drive where I crossed US 12. 63.4 323.6
23 April A nice and easy route toward Paoli. Out Seminole to Sunset Drive and back Walen, Fitchrona, Nesbit, to the bike bridge via Lovell Lane. I think returning on Whitney Way to Hammersley to the bike bridge would be better but perhaps longer. 24.3 347.9
26 April WNR, Riley. Dan, Jack, and I were deviation demons. Even though Jack and I cut off the last part of the route, our early deviations resulted in a total mileage equal to the official route. The temperature was mild and the air was incredibly clear. 22.2 370.1
29 April We did a ride not too unlike this one. There was a bit a gravel first to avoid and then to tollerate but all in all an okay route. The Railroad Inn Resturant in North Freedom was a classic small town family resturant except for one thing, it was completely non-smoking. Freedom road was the highlight of the trip (in more ways than one). 51.9 422
3 May Festge Park, WNR with Jack, Barb, Dan, and Oliver. It was very warm starting out in the sun but cooled nicely when the sun hid behind a large cool for awhile. I felt strong on the hills. 22 444
6 May Pat and I did a loop to the south which included a section of the new Capital City Bike trail. 14 458
7 May Bombay's Indian Lake Ride: I was a lovely day. I extended the long ride slightly by pedalling into Mezzo for lunch. I had a nice talk with John Rider who is going to do his own cross country tour over the summer. He's starts in Portland OR in three weeks and today was his longest training ride. We wish him well! 67.3 525.3
10 May WNR, Oregon. Dan, Oliver, and I did the Wednesday Night Ride. This ride is mostly flat with a few nice hills. It was calm as we headed south and west but later a strong wind from the south helped us on the return. 35 560.3
13 May RTR Training Ride: Jack, Dan, Dennis, Evan, Adam, & I headed out from Jack's house near Black Earth. It was cold! It was really windy (15-30 MPH). It was also really hilly but I think the hills helped us against the wind. I biked back to Madison with that strong tail wind. At home, wine and morrels comprised a sweet reward. 77.4 637.7
20 May We did the RTR group ride to Blanchardville without the Blanchards. It was an absolutely beautiful ride and the pedalling was easier than in years past do to perfect weather and light winds. 78.4 716.1
24 May I didn't leave work until it was too late to make the WNR so I did a city ride. 18.4 734.5
28 May Rockies training ride to Mineral Point: Lunch at the brew pub on Commerce Street was fabulous. I had the soup, walnut burger, and fries. The jalapeno poppers were very well done. The brews were good and the atomosphere of the old stone building was charming. Before and after lunch we biked on the dead quiet roads of Iowa County. The beauty of Mounds View road was complemented by a great blue. It was a cool gray day with a bit of a head wind heading back. 78 812.5
3 June The group biked from Paoli to Evansville for lunch. I biked from Madison to Paoli and to took a rather long way back from Paoli (know that I'd have to work today). It was easy, flat, and good to see that the old bridge on Leedlemill Road still stands. 85.4 897.9
7 June Pat did her first long ride on a WNR. Biking slower allows for longer views of the hills and the setting sun. It was also fun to meet up and bike with some old biking friends like Judy Strausser, Margret Peterson, and Don Katz. Judy is retiring from WPR in a few weeks and moving to Taos to write poetry about science. It kinda makes you think, nes pas? As far as my training goes, I'm feeling confident yet under trained. I've got a lot of miles in but unlike most years I've not been running at all. I feel strong enough that I know I'll have a good time. 26 923.9

Total Miles: 923.9
