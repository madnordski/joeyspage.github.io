Bicycling Community Page Training Log, 1999
by Joseph King and Scott Rose

Joe

Last year's training log.

DateDescriptionMilesTotal
26 February Around lake Wingra on the way home from work. I was nearly dead on my feet at work so I left early. Even so I found the idea of a ride through the arboretum impossible to resist. 10 10
3 March Today I put 11 miles on my odometer. 3 miles, then work, 2 miles then lunch, 2 miles then work, 2 miles then dinner and beers, 2 miles then home. So all in all it could be a net training loss but what the heck, I'll give myself 2. 2 12
8 March A trip around lake Wingra and to lunch at Gino's. The new bike overpass of the beltline gives me access to some really good sub sandwiches. Unfortunately, they didn't blow the path after last Saturday's snow fall so I had to bike across a golf course fairway. The veggie sub comes with peppers and artichokes. 10 22
10 March This a bike ride to ski for lunch. I attached my skis to my bike and cycled to Owen Park. In the last 24 hours I've skied the longest trails at Blue Mounds, Elver Park, and Owen Park. If that sounds like a lot, don't be too impressed it only amounts to 12 miles or so. 3 25
14 March This could be my first real training ride of the year. It's the first ride over 20 miles, and I used my touring bike instead of my commuter. It was a beautiful sunny day. The ride went west out Old Sauk to Twin Valley to U.S. 14 to Rocky Dell to Airport Rd. to Browner Rd. to Schneider and then back east to U.S. 12 to Graber to High St. to Gammon to Old Sauk and home. 26.4 51.4
17 March 43 miles on my 43rd birthday. I did the Black Earth Pie Ride in reverse this time. I had cherry pie that has never seen the inside of a refrigerator with a nice flakey crust. The filling was out of a can, of course. With winds over 20 mph the ride out was real work but the ride back well that was a joy. I felt like a bullet slicing through the air. 43.4 94.8
21 March Out to Cottage Grove via Femrite Rd. and back via Buckeye Road. These are two of Madison's few remaining escape routes. Saddly, however, these roads aren't enough like Mineral Point to make city officials happy so they are slated for four-laning. 38.9 133.7
29 March Lunch time ride to Grandview Rd., south of Madison. 16.8 150.5
30 March Today I made it further south to Cty M on my lunch hour. 19 169.5
31 March Picnic in the arb with Pat. 9 178.5
4 April What a beautiful day for a ride. I heard and then saw a ring neck pheasant. A young dear watched me struggle up a hill, and I saw a raccoon taking a dump in a pasture. 54.9 233.4
7 April Biked to Paoli to meet up with the Wednesday Night Ride Gang. It was very, very windy heading down. Dan and I started out on the ride together and then I headed off for home when we hit Fritz Rd. Frogs and Pheasants provided the musical accompaniment to the ride. 34.4 267.8
10 April How wonderful it was to have sunny skies and great company for today's ride. I spent most of the ride looking for cranes but the best I could do was two Canadian geese. I took a detour from Pasqual's to home to avoid Old Middleton Road. Last year I cracked a rim on that road. 40.3 308.1
13 April A fast and easy ride toward Paoli over my lunch hour. 16.3 324.4
14 April Belville WNR: Rode with Dan, Rodger, and Mariane. About 90 riders showed up for a beautiful ride and some views of a flying crane. 25.6 350
17 April Cold, wind, and talk of wine. It air was exceptionally clear even though the skies were not. No rain but a stiff breeze and a couple of fine long hills. Everyone seemed especially strong today. Dan saved both Evan and myself by outfitting us with some much needed gear. 56.4 406.4
28 April Dan, Jack, and I had a lovely WNR. The sky was blue for the Riley ride this year. Dan treated us to an extra long hill and some nice views of the area. Along the route a couple of cranes and a wild turkey came out to view the parade of cyclists. 24 430.4
1 May A broken spoke cut short one of my favorite rides of the year. I'll have to read Jack's report to find out what was good for lunch in New Glarus. 38 468.4
9 May It was a route similar to last year's with some variation. Less Iowa County but more of Sauk. Except for Jack, we all started the ride in Sauk City. Dan and Dennis ending there, Evan, Adam, Jack, and myself biking to our homes. What the ride lacked in pie it made up for in clear skies and clean air. Much to our disappointment, Plain Jane has closed her doors to business. We headed on to Spring Green but instead of pie we found soup at our stop there. We took the flat route just north of US14 from Cty C to Arena. That skipped about three ridges and helped to keep the mileage under 90 on a day I had planned to do 65. 84 552.4
22 May Lunch at the Taliensin Visitor Center was not the fine dinning we expected. Fortunately, the pizza and chips were a perfect match for our biking attire. The hills of Iowa County on, Zwettler, Meadow Vale, Ridge View, Oak, Dugway, Cty T, Golf Course, Coon Rock, Amacher Hollow, and Old Indian trail helped balance out the calories consumed nicely. We found the summer flowers of Goats Beard, Bladder Wort and Cow Parsnip beginning to flower while the spring show of Woodland Geraniums, Columbines, Lupines, and Danes Rocket were still going strong. Adam, Dan, Dennis, Evan, Jack, and myself enjoyed the views and the cool light breeze along the way. 55.9 608.3
26 May Dan, Dennis, Jack, and I did the Salmo Pond WNR. It was absolutely beautiful. We lost Jack when we followed the old route on Forshaug instead of taking Moe to Greenwald. Dan pealed off on J and Dennis and I finished the ride with a diversion to Barlow Road. I hit 54 MPH which is an all time high for me in Wisconsin. My Colorado high stands at 55, I think. 26.5 634.8
29 May Dan, Dennis, Jack and I headed out from Dan's for pie in Blanchardville. Once again the restaurant was out of business. It was a beautiful ride down to Blanchardville on a perfect late spring day. We enjoyed a nice dessert at a coffee shop after lunch in a bar. I was a very satisfying ride. 69 703.8
30 May I did the Daleyville Dip with Dave and the Bombay Bicycle Club. Dave, who I met during the ride, broke his front derailer cable which only made him faster up the hills. 57.4 761.2
1 June Pat and I enjoy morning rides out Seminole Highway. These are good recovery rides for me. 23.2 784.4
3 June Pat and I did our morning ride out Seminole Highway. 13.2 797.6
4 June A hot and humid ride to Mineral Point for lunch. The usual suspects (A,D,D,E,J,J) plus Dave. We were kind to ourselves this year taking a less hilly route out and we found a new more pleasant return route that takes us west of Mineral Point on County QQ & Q. After lunch the heat took it's toll on all of us with possible exception of Dave and Dennis who were looking for more hills to climb on the return route. 85 882.6
6 June A late start (10am) meant another hot ride but it did prove one thing, I'm ready. I felt OK after yesterday's tough ride on this flat ride to Paoli and around Verona. I attribute these good feelings to good drinking habits mostly. Even though today's ride was short, I had no trouble draining two large water bottles. 34.4 917
9 June An easy ride on which Pat pointed out all of the flowers I've been misidentifying all spring. I had a flat on my first and last Conti. 18 935
4 July Rockies was as great as always but I never seemed to bike really hard this year. It's that I was in such great shape, I just didn't push as I have in the past. Oh well, there's always next year. An after the Rockies ride out south and west. I biked harder and faster than anything I did during RTR, heading out Midtown, dropping down to Verona on Wood and Nine Mound, and headed back on Cty J/Mauer/Old Sauk. When all was said and done my average was 16.1 at my door. 32 967

Total Miles: 967
