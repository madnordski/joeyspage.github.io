1 December This was a run to camp Randall and back, 45:56 (8.5 min/mile). The route picks up the SW Path at Glenway and takes it to Breeze Tr. The return is on Kendall. 5.4 11.8
3 March The perfect first ride. Although the temperature was not supposed to be above 40 the sun made it feel much warmer. 14 25.8
4 March Our Seminole Hwy route again today with one additional element -- WIND! It was also below freezing which was a good test for our cold weather gear. It looks like we'll have to make one more trip to the bike store. 14 39.8
6 March To Paoli by the usual route: Seminole - Walen - Fitchberg - Borchert - Percell - Sayles Tr. - Sun Valley. The return is also well worn: Range Tr. - Cty M - Walen - Seminole. It was brilliantly sunny with temps near 30. 26.1 65.9
7 March To Cross Plains: Grand Canyon, McKena, Raymond, Gammon, Midtown, Timber, Old Sauk Tr, Stagecoach, Cty P and back on Cty P & Airport. It was a bit of a struggle heading out against the wind and it got really cold when cloud cover rolled in unexpectedly. I was thankful that the wind-assisted return route was short and fast. 32.6 98.5
9 March To Verona: Seminole, Grandview (VRoman), Cty PB, Wahlen, Cty M, Locust, Hwy 69, Riverside, Sugar River, Valley, Hwy 69, Cty M, Cross Country, Nesbit, Fitchrona, Lacy, Seminole. I made a few wrong turns and explored a couple of dead ends, which is always fun until the dogs come running at you. Another brillantly sunny day with temps just above freezing. (15.6) 30.5 129
11 March Ride to Paoli and back. We took the same direct route out and back. 26.0 155
17 March Old Sauk -> Cross Plains -> Festge Park -> Table Bluff -> KP -> Otto Kerl -> Enchanted Valley -> Schiender -> return via Airport Rd. Temps in the upper 30's under sunny skies with a light north wind. I have a new rule -- nothing over 30 in when the temp is in the 30's -- my feet were just to damn cold at the end of this ride. 38.9 193.9
18 March The Paoli WNR route with Pat. It was wonderfully sunny. 20.1 214
20 March I met Dan in Paoli and we biked past Oregon and back. (15.1) 48.2 262.2
21 March We did the Belleville WNR route. 17.8 280
27 March Verona & back this time without any wrong turns. Nice and sunny ~38 degrees. (16.9) 28.2 308.2
28 March Cross Plains, this time out Scheinder and back Old Sauk. A strong south wind with an occasional ray of sun. My legs were reluctant on the hills because of yesterday's hard ride. (15.1) 28.3 336.5
31 March What a perfect day for our first ride. We cut off the hill on Cty A by taking Cty U. Otherwise, it was the ride as planned. After a day of skiing (the day following this ride), my legs are exhusted. 22 358.5
7 April We went biking and nobody died. We did get a cut ankle and a fat lip, however. Next time the wind is in excess of the 50 MPH, I think I might have to clean my office instead. 27.1 385.6
8 April Seminole Hwy: A nice day. 13.5 399.1
10 April Madison to Monticello. Madison -> Paoli -> PB to Belleville -> WNR in reverse except on the return I stayed on Argue to Fritz -> Locust -> Nesbit -> Lacy -> Seminole. It was a beautiful day for a ride (16.4) 63.7 462.8
11 April The Belleville WNR. Joe's rain rule #4 applied so Pat and I loaded ourselves and bikes into the car during a constant rain. When we got to Belleville the sun came out and the wind picked up. We cut the ride short not wishing to replicate last Saturday's ride. Still it felt good to get out. 13.7 476.5
13 April The smell of sun screen, the cold dry air, the bright sun, and the banana hazard on the road combined to make this mornings ride feel like a morning during Ride the Rockies. I was especially surprised when waiting for the light to change at Cty PD and Seminole three cyclists came up behind us and one asked, "Are you Joe? Joe King?" It turned to be Dave from Crono Metro. I've never seen or talked to him before but he recognized the wheels he built for my Serotta and amazingly enough recalled my name. When the light changed, Pat then led the five of us out in a paceline, pedaling smoothly in her new clipless pedals. 16.5 493
14 April Paoli to New Glarus with the RTR Group. It was fun, fun, and more fun to ride with all ten of us doing great. We had plenty of hills, sun, and sky. Absent but not missed was the wind and mechanical failures. Our group broke into to two for a moment when I directed Adam, David, Evan, and Jack toward Mount Veron. The portabella mushroom burger at the Glarner Stube was good but the onion stayed with me all the way back to Paoli. 38.6 531.6
15 April The hollandaise sauce, egg, and crab cake flavors combined so perfectly it reminded me of France. Orange juice is no substitute for rose wine but this in Bluphies of Madison and not some French cafe in Carpentras. Oh yea, we also had a wonderfully quiet ride out Seminole where we designed experiments for determining which bike rolls best on the downhills of Fitchrona and Lacy roads. 16.4 548
18 April Climbed a few hills (Capital View, Enchanted Valley, Hill Point (from the base and over the top), Otto Kern, Schuman. (15.8) 38.8 586.8
20 April The veggi melt was positively yummy but so large that I wasn't tempted by the pie afterwards. This despite the fact that had 35 miles under us before sitting down to lunch. The wind was calm in the morning but picked up to inhibit our return to Madison from Lodi. The sun also came out later in the day with temps topping 70 degrees. 59.0 645.8
21 April Festge Park to Lodi with the RTR group (Jack, Dan, & Barb): I stopped carrying a chain tool about three years ago and today was first time since I started biking that I could have used one. Barb caught her chain quite badly between the frame and the chainrings. Rather than scratch her frame forcing it out, I biked the 1/2 mile to the car and retrieved her and her bike. She headed off to Trek for repairs. It was windy coming back but a lovely day in all. The pie made it all worthwhile. 45.3 691.1
22 April I was hanging with the lead group and was taking a short turn at the front climbing a small rise when I flatted. This was the Sunday Bombay ride and it looked like I was going to be able to hang with the fast group for the remaining six miles into Cambridge until the flat. Two riders, one after the other, stopped and thanked me for giving them an excuse to drop off or end the always futile attempt to reattach. In Cambridge I met up with Dave Peterson and Gail. We had a bite to eat while Gail told me a few stories about her Oregon to Madison trek she did many years ago. The three of us pedalled home with a nice breeze at our back. On the way home I stopped at Dotty's for what will surely be the last time at that location. There is something especially good about their vegi-burgers. 62.8 753.9
27 April Verona & back. This is the same ride as before except the in-town route now uses Westmooreland (18.1). It's fun to see a progression on this route with average speeds climbing from 15.6(3/9), 16.9(3/27), to today's 18.1. Near the end of the ride it felt like I had reached the end of my fitness but not the end of legs. Surprisingly, I wasn't tired afterward. 28.5 782.4
28 April What a great group of riders we are! We started at Sauk City and took in the Baraboo hills in grand style. Here is the Cue Sheet. After several steep and long hills before lunch, we were all ready to eat at Carol's Railroad Diner. Carol told me that she wasn't sure if we were coming or if she had dreamed the conversation we had the night before. I called the restaurant the night before at about 11 PM expecting an answering machine but I apparently awoke the proprietor from a sound sleep instead. We did three very significant climbs before lunch -- Freedom Rd. to Happy Hill, Terry Town to Hogs Back Rd., and Rock Springs to North Freedom. I knew these would be long climbs but I was surprised by how many steep sections we encountered. To have completed this ride is to have done well. 57.5 839.9
29 April Pat insisted that we do a ride as hard or harder than the group ride on Saturday so I unwittingly opted for harder. Madison -> Cross Plains -> Mt. Horrible (via) the ridges and the reverse via the windy valleys (3,400 feet of climbing). Surprisingly there was a morale zapping head wind from Black Earth to Madison even though most of the wind was out of the south. We managed it somehow. 68.2 908.1
1 May Out Seminole to spin out the kinks. 13.4 921.5
2 May Rain Rule #2 states, "if it looks like rain when you're starting out but isn't raining, RIDE. I've had many wonderful (and dry) rides by following this rule. I can remember days when the air was so calm it was scary and other rides under the most dramatic skies you've ever seen." Once again we enjoyed a beautiful ride by following this rule. 23 944.5
5 May Cold and rain cut this training ride short. Very nice bumbleberry pie at the Blue Ski restaurant in Mt. Horeb. 20 964.5
6 May Gov Dodge -> Avoca -> Spring Green -> Gov. Dodge: We escaped the rain by driving west to do this ride on roads my bike have never seen before. Iowa County is just the best place on earth to ride a bicycle. Only one dog came onto the road after us and it bit me. There was no skin broken. 56.3 1020.8
8 May It's an 850 foot climb from the creek crossing on Ryan Rd. to the Parking lot for the west tower at Blue Mound State Park. I started my charge of the top of Forshaug Rd. too soon and barely crawled to the top in my lowest gear. An Oriole danced past my head just before the climb of Fasenfeld. The numbers tell a tale, 5,010 feet of climbing, 15 mph ave speed, 48 max speed in only 67 miles. Some one was reading my Rockies web page and was asking advice from a fellow "flat lander." I'm not so sure that I qualify. 67.2 1088
11 May Michael and Keith's Friday Morning Foray. 27.9 1115.9
12 May A ride cut short. I was frightened when Pat experienced the rapid onset of a severe headache. 9 1124.9
13 May Destination Oregon: We had perfect weather for this ride to Oregon. Heading south on Story Town road we were treated to views of both pheasant and crane. It was fun to see two baby cranes scampering to keep up with mom & dad. The few nice hills added up to 2,680 feet of climbing. It was a good ride which we finished with energy to spare. A most satisfying lunch was had at the Bronx cafe on Main street in Oregon. 50.5 1175.4
14 May Thunderstorms had me do an about face on my way to Paoli this morning. I was very cold and very wet for the entire ride back to home. 21 1196.4
15 May 3 Turkeys, 2 Mounts, and some nice "views." This unplanned ride turned out to be Madison -> Verona -> Mt. Vernon -> Mt. Horeb -> Iowa County -> Black Earth -> Madison. Pat was with me when the first turkey emerged from a white van to strut his chest in my face. The van came within inches of my handle bar as we started away from the light and he turned right in front of us so I stupidly tapped the side of the van with my hand. I'm sure he managed to learn nothing from the exchange. The second turkey was waving his long neck as he walked along in the woods. The third turkey nearly frightened me right off the bike as he took to flight just as I passed him on the side of the road near the top off Zwettler. Pinnacle Rd. is still in really bad shape but Ridge View and Lake View Roads were fabulous. Bryn Grwyn has lots of loose gravel on top of pavement and is a bit rough. This amounted to 4990 feet of climbing. The southern approach to Mt. Horeb must be flatter than the route that passes through Riley. 81.4 1277.8
16 May Brigham Park WNR: It was an absolutely beautiful ride. The columbine was out in force and so were the bugs which prevented us from hanging around at the end of the ride. We did, however, catch a wonderful sunset on the way home. 23.1 1300.9
18 May See http://www.kingresearch.com/jk/biking/MadisonToSeattle/field-test.htm. 106.9 1407.8
19 May Following the northern branch of the old military road we ended up in Poynette for lunch. The Memory Lane Restaurant (where I've eaten in past years) is now closed for good. My streak of closing restaurants in small Wisconsin towns continues. Look out Iowa, Nebraska, Wyoming, Montana, Canada, and Washington -- I'm headed your way. 62.6 1470.4
20 May A repeat of the Oregon ride with some variations. The Vegi-melt at the Bronx Diner was a vast improvement over the portabella mushroom burger of last week. We completed our 110 mile training weekend with energy to spare. 49.1 1519.5
23 May Another great WNR this time from Indian Lake. Rain loomed but all we ever felt were a few sprinkles. We were treated to an absolutely wonderous sky. It was cool, 50's or lower -- it was a mistake not to wear the shoe covers. 26.9 1546.4
26 May The Mineral Point Ride with the RTR group minus the Blanchards. Everything is now in bloom, from the Black Locust trees to the Spider Wort. Lunch in Mineral Point was wonderful eventhough the key lime pie was not at all tart. The jalapeno poppers were, once again, superb. Rain on the way back made the later appearance of the sun an absolute joy. 78.7 1625.1
28 May I joined the Blanchards who were on a mission to get an 80 mile ride in today. We planned the route as we went along and succeeded in creating a superb ride. Madison -> Lodi -> Gibraltar Rock -> Merrimac Ferry -> School House for Lunch -> Ferry -> Fish & Crystal Lakes -> Wollersheim Winery -> Dane -> Madison. Everyone did extremely well which seals the deal -- all 10 of us are now ready for Rockies! 93.7 1718.8
30 May Salmo Pond WNR with all but Barb & Dan. It was fun (and I'll use that word a lot) biking with the group on this slightly modified ride (Forschaug instead of Greenwald & Barlow instead of Garfoot). It was fun talking with Kathy Wauk at the top of JJ as we recounted the time she helped me back to Madison at the end of my first fully loaded tour. It was also fun to see Dennis and Adam playing on the hills at the head of the group -- these guys are super strong this year. Finally, it was really fun to see Pat enjoying herself on such a hilly WNR. 26.1 1744.9
2 June Seminole Highway to try out my new shoes for my trip to Seattle. 13.4 1758.3
3 June Graham's Old Mill Restaurant in Cambridge started Pat & I off with a complimentary grapefruit which was grilled with sugar and cinnamon. My brunch included sweet potatoes grilled with red bell peppers and onion as the side to the breakfast "tarta." The tarta was caramelized onions with yellow squash and peppers in a pastry cup. Along with the additional side of a fruit cup, I enjoyed it thoroughly. Although, as Pat pointed out on the ride home, a simple and more efficient lunch might be a better compliment to 80 miles of biking. In biking from Madison to Cambridge the best parts were the Capital City Trail and East Church Road out of Rockdale. In fact, the Stoughton area seems to have an abundance of discourteous motorists. Nothing blatant or hostile toward us directly, just a general unwillingness to slow down the little least bit. Iowa County is, in fact, a biker's paradise. 80.1 1838.4
6 June Lodi WNR with Evan, Adam, & Pat. It was cool and damp but very pleasant overall. We had good conversation among us and with Margret Peterson who is also doing a Colorado ride this year of their own making. 26.8 1865.2
8 June The Nearby Driftless 50: Summer finally arrived and it is wonderful -- no arm warmers for the first time in weeks! This route makes the most of the driftless area closest to Madison -- I'll have to document it so that Pat can do it to keep sharp while I'm gone. The route has a very pleasant start out Seminole over to Vroman (Grandview) -> PB -> Whalen -> M -> Locust -> Hyw. 69 -> Riverside -> Sugar River -> G -> Dairy Ridge -> White Crossing -> Timber -> Paulson -> J (Riley) -> Riley-Klevenville -> Valley Spring -> J -> Garfoot -> Mineral Point -> Barlow -> Garfoot -> Braun -> Union Valley -> KP -> Bourbon (XPlains) -> P -> Stagecoach -> Old Sauk Trail -> Timber -> Old Sauk -> Old Middleton. I hit 51.6 on Barlow and I had a good accent on Braun which has a sustained double digit grade and a nice curve on the downhill. When I started out I felt as though I'd lost a good deal of strength in the last few weeks and about 16 miles into the ride my back and my left knee were killing me. I backed off a bit and when I got to Riley I lowered the saddle just a tad (I had moved it back a bit before the ride today). Wow! Everything was good after that. In fact I averaged 17.0 m.p.h. for entire trip. BTW: all of my recorded averages for rides that begin and end at home have been from backdoor to backdoor. 52.6 1917.8
9 June RTR Group (minus Dennis, Adam, & Barb) ride to New Glarus starting in Paoli. I didn't eat well in New Glarus and finished dehydrated and out of gas. I did enjoy the new and extremely quiet roads that the exploring on the return route revealed. I'd really like to just load up the car and head off tomorrow -- after a good long sleep, that is. 44.6 1962.4
11 June Paoli and back. Everything feels good. (18.0) 26 1988.4
