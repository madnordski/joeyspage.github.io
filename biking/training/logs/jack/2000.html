Bicycling Community Page Training Log, 2000
by Joseph King and Scott Rose

Jack

Last Year's Log

DateDescriptionMiles Total
27 February It was 50 degrees,sunny and breezy when I spontaneously decided to go for a spin. The roads had been washed by a downpour last Thursday. My bike only needed tire inflation, an easy matter. After ascending Ryan Rd. to the Park, I turned back, not wanting to over-do it. After decending, I climbed Moyer Rd and Blue Mounds Trail on my return. A good start to a promising season. 20 20
4 March Mid-sixties, sunny, calm. Rode FF/KK to K, then Roelke to Knight Hollow to HH to Ridge View, Up Mounds Park and home via Ryan Rd. Good ride, good surfaces. 26 46
5 March Lethargic this morning, presumeably from yesterday's workout. When the temperature hit 70, I went out to do previous ride in reverse. After descending Mounds Park, decided to go straight back via K and over Zwettler. Phenominal weather. What one would hope-for for the first weekend in May, not March. 20 66
23 March Just enough time before dark for a counterclockwise loop into eastern Iowa Co. FF/KK to K to Zwettler, up the easy side and down the steep side, then straight home. Pleasant! 14 80
26 March Met Barb for an encore of last fall's ride to Lodi. Enjoyed a nice tailwind on the way there. It subsided while we feasted on homemade scalloped potatoes, good!,but picked up fiercely against our progress as we neared Middleton. We were trying, but the headwind cost us an extra hour on the return trip. I was brow beat into climbing Black Hill Road, but it wasn't bad. Highlight of ride: matinee performance by two pair of Sandhill over and into Lodi Marsh. Lovely. 40 120
1 April E&A, Barb,Joe, Dan and me south from Dan's to Wittwer,west to Primrose Ctr. and return thru Mt. Vernon. Glad we took the hill on J--it's a sweet pull. Good to be out with the group again. 22 142
2 April Met barb and her friends on Stagecoach Road nesr quarry. Back to the Lunch Bucket for second breakfast. Had to work that off by riding up through Mazo and on to Roxbury. Headwinds were the story after that. Spring Valley and Turkey road to home. Found the scenes along these two roads lovely at this time of year. 52 194
9 April A stiff wind from the north-northwest kept us from setting any speed records until about 4 miles from the finish when we were too tired to care. Barb and I did a Middleton to Mt Horeb to Black Earth to Middleton loop under a warm sun but chilly air. After a meatball and mashed potatoes at Shuberts, we found climbs on Blue Mounds Trail, Moen Valley Road and Enchanted Valley Road. No problem for us, though we did have to be cautious on the downsides due to heavy sanding during Friday's snowfall. 47 241
13 April Late afternoon turned sunny and warm with light winds. Rode counter-clock loop into Iowa Co. Climbs: Sutcliff, Zwettler and Blue Mounds Trail. Got home with "runner's high", wishing I had planned a longer loop. 14 255
15 April Cross Plains to Dan's to Glarner Stube (my choice: vegetable + Swiss soup and Portabella + cheddar on bun was just right) to X-Plains left me exhausted. I have a lot more riding to do before I feel ready for seven days in the mountains. 56 311
22 April Best riding weather since early March. Rode to Festge to meet group. Lunch at Lodi. Favorite views were from Vanness Rd. (river valley and B. Range) and from Beuthin Rd. (of glacial ponds). Felt good, but not dying to ride again Sunday. 60 371
25 April Though I must say, the ride through Middleton's business park and the early turn that dumped us onto Old Sucks Rd at 5:15 was a true drag, the rest of our ride to, of all places, the Verona Truck Stop for very satisfactory beef stew and fresh strawberry pie, was really quite lovely. Riding past all those huge, new homes up on Blackhawk on our return reminded me of the reality that my home is fairly humble by comparison and is a potential castle mostly in my own imagination. 26 mi is a "piece of pie" after a few of more than 50. 26 397
26 April Added to my fond memories of this ride--the Riley Loop. 22 miles in less than 1:30, ideal weather. That last hill on Timber Lane is steep! Just Joe, Dan and me. Took 3 deliberate variations, but did not lead anyone else off course this year. 22 419
30 April My word, Joe sure delivered a terrific variation on our annual Sauk County Pie Ride. From the fertile river valley, up and over the very "private" Baraboo Range, then out to the west, north and east again where the land is mostly high and open. A wonderful drop into the North Freedom area. The town features a respectable "Railroad Restaurant." The return route on Freedom Road was even better, having that private feel from beginning to end. Some areas should never be allowed to change. Would that this be one of them. 52 471
1 May Modest hills and managed thrills were the essence of this after work outing. Zwettler, K, Meadow Vale, Ridge View, Mounds Park. Returned via Ryan and Blue Mounds Trail. Enjoyed limiting my speeds on my descents by sitting tall and letting the air save my brakes. You gotta love them hills! 20 491
3 May Rode from home to join the Festge Park WNR--a warm one at first. Did the route with Barb. Ride home felt good. I pressed and got home between sunset and darkness. Looking forward to Saturday. 37 528
6 May Yet another fine Saturday training ride, this one from home to Mt. Horeb to New Glarus and back. Though the last 11 miles were downhill with the wind, they still depleted me of energy. I need more of these longer rides to try to reduce my recovery time. 70 598
10 May Though the sky threatened us the whole time, it mustered only a few spits. Barb and I decided to do an alternate WNR from Middleton, out Blackhawk, Old Sauk etc. and returning Enchanted Valley and Schneider. HIGHlights: for the hay of it, substation hill on Stagecoach, then a really nice climb on quiet Hilltop Rd. Cake. 25 623
13 May With full group less Barb, this ride sought out the significant climbs from my house to Blue Mound to Pleasant Ridge to the Wright Visitors Center. After lunch, it was about 22 miles back, the shortest route I know across the ridges to home. The headwinds on the way out were on the edge of demoralizing. 56 679
14 May Riley Loop with variations with Barb. Weather better than Saturday's, but wind still fairly stiff from the West. 20 699
20 May It had been cold overnight, but our day was pleasant and sunny, with light winds. Excellent conditions. A beautiful, quiet route both to and from Blanchardville from Dan's. Views from F outstanding. RTR group less E&A. Lunch tasted good, but not heart-healthy. Zorba would not approve. 62 761
23 May Despite lethargy, after work took on Zwettler, Meadow Vale, Mounds Park and Blue Mounds Trail. Loved the descents, notably Ryan Road. 22 783
25 May Out and back the most direct route to west tower, Blue Mound S.P. Warm & sunny. 21 804
28 May Saturday's ride a day late. I liked this route thru Blue Mound, then alternating south and west roads to Mineral Point. Found a brew-pub that lives up to expectations. What a nice place to be on a cool, cloudy and breezy afternoon. Missed like the plague last year's heat and humidity. 77 881
3 June Paoli--Bad Fish Creek Bridge--Evansville (factory pie)--Paoli with full contingent. Clear, cool, mostly sunny. A nice change of scene and pace, but not our usual taxing workout. 48 929
6 June Responding to a so-far faulty weather forecast that indicated that Tuesday would likely be the only fair weather day this week, companion and I took holiday this afternoon. At about 2 PM, we left my place on a figure 8 tour with Dodgeville our supper stop. This was a hell of a nice ride and the weather WAS perfect. The common part of the routes out and back was from Hyde to Y to ZZ. The 36 mile route to supper included Blue Mounds Trail, Ryan Rd, Ridge View and ZZ. The return was via the famous roller coaster road, Sectionline Rd, east from Dodgeville to Y. From there, we simply repeated our return with the full group from Mineral Point via Evans Quarry Rd. Just two climbs remained after leaving Mill Creek Valley--Roulke Rd and Blue Mounds Trail. Lady luck frowned on us as we pedalled in near darkness after 9:00, two turns from home. I turned up blue Mounds Trail to the left after the tricky triangle intersection, and companion turned right before it up the hill to the David Dybdahl's. I soon went back looking for said companion, but on Pleasant Valley, not BMT to the south. Rides are like that sometimes! We did not kill each other either. 69 998

Total Miles: 998
