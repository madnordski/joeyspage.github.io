3 March Spontaneous beautiful weather. Dropped what I was doing, inflated tires on older Holdsworth, rode to X-Plains and back. Experienced "rubber" legs on Old Indian Trail hill. 18 18
9 March A Friday off due to floor sanding at Tom's this weekend. Sunny, warm, light wind. Off to Mathew's in Cross Plains for soup and sandwich served on sunporch. Way to live. Rubber legs on hill did not repeat. Specialized saddle on Klein a big improvement over that on old bike. 18 36
17 March Cty. F to Ryan Road. Turned back after steep stretch wanting to spin more than stress this early in the season and wanting to want to ride again Sunday when the weather is forecasted to be even better. Did climb the hills an Blue Mounds Trail from the south. 14 50
18 March Pleasant Valley, F, Iowa K, Mounds Park to skiers parking lot. Ice field remains at this altitude, I was surprised, but no skiers, no surprise there. Home via Ryan and BMT. Feel great & slept soundly. I can do this. 23 73
31 March From Riley to Dan's to meet for first group ride. Sunny, warm for March. Dan's south to Witwer, west to U, north to Mt. Vernon and (for me) back to Riley. Hill on J up to Military Ridge is a favorite of mine. At end of ride, I got the hang of my new STI shifters. The control for the front triple was tricky to learn. I had forced a double down-shift, kicking the chain onto the bottom bracket shell, putting me in neutral on the sharp rise before Dairy Ridge. Sleepy but happy and satisfied durning snow this afternoon. 24 97
4 April Sunny,low 50s. To Cross Plains, Shelton's, for soup & sandwich. North on P and Enchanted Valley to K. West on K and Spring Valley to F. Thence to home. Good woorkout. Stiff wind from the east first hindered, then helped. 25 122
7 April I don't really know how far I went, but distance and hills were not the story anyway. It was a the breeze, straight drom the south when I started out from Pine Bluff at 9:10. As time went by, the breeze became even stronger, switching slightly to from the south-southwest. Barb and I tried to repeat last week's ride, but got all but blown into the farm fields along Cty A. When we got our feet on the ground, a tremendous gust blew our bikes into our right calves, causing a black, greasy, bloody mess from the sharp teeth of our outer chainrings. We turned back, as I feared an even worse mishap had we tried to brave it out to Cty U. This was a good workout, but on the south side of real fun. 25 147
10 April Home to BMSP park entrance and return. Nice ride in short sleeves. Enjoyed a run on the trail with Stoker yesterday. Will cross-train remainder of the month, at least. 20 167
13 April A near repeat of a previous ride--to Sheltons for soup and sandwich, then north to K, then to home. Lovely, sunny, cool day with light winds. This time I climbed Hilltop Rd. from P, and took the Turkey RD. alternative from Spring Valley adding hills but saving a mile. 24 191
14 April Can you imagine? the whole bunch, all ten of us RTR folks! A perfect day for the ride from Paoli to New Glarus and return. The Biercheese soup at Glarner Stube was outstanding. The turkey melt was OK too. Early Spring in southern Wisconsin is spectacular and riding with this bunch is special. Spring Rose Road with a 1/4 face wind was the best. 36 227
18 April After lunch at the University Club, (with PMD, JG, MS, RH, a truly disimilar group--I worked for them all) west on Zwetler, up Meadow Vale, then Mounds Park, then home via BMT. Down Ryan Rd was a blast. My right leg is talking to me in ways I don't like, but who says training is painless. Weather was cool, but sunny with moderate winds from the W-NW. 23 250
20 April Rode from X-Plains to Dan Atkins'and back. Picked up new Fly-Ti saddle. Complained of weak down-shifting to small chainwheel. Diagnosis: Ultegra STI levers not quite compatible with Campy Triple front. Changed to Ultegra: problem solved. VIP treatment, no waiting, no service charge. 22 272
21 April Happy about putting in 58 miles in quite windy conditions and feeling good afterwards. Left home at 9:20 and was the first to arrive at Festge Park. Barb, Joe, Dan and I did this ride (in a reverse or clockwise pattern) to the "over the creek" restraurant in interesting Lodi. Very interesting. Watched Suzie the duck get lusted for and fought over by about nine drakes as we waited to be served. I know almost nothing about such matters. She "tried" to escape, but the three-foot creek wall provided her excuse for failure. Poor thing. She is a cutie, as ducks go, I guess. If someone scored, they were quick about it, end of commentary. I gather we all liked our food--three of us had blueberry-dutch pie made with fresh-frozen BBs. It was on the high side of good. The ride up to Lodi with the wind was such a piece of cake, I didn't deserve pie. The ride back against the strong face to cross winds was taxing. Winds begone! 58 330
24 April Pulled garlic mustard this morning and enjoyed it. Ate it as I went along and it's delicious. Plan to mix it in with a Magic Mill salad mix next time I have the chance. Ride was out Knight Hollow, over to H, then up Blue Ridge (lovely), then HH (this part's a skyway between two dells), Ridge View, Mounds Park, Ryan, BMT, home. 2 hrs and 40 min. Fun ride, sunny and cool. 29 359
27 April Beautiful day, light winds from the northeast. Goal was lunch at Spring Green. Mushroom Straganoff? at General Store/Cafe. Excellent! Went out Knight Hollow, Peterson, thru Arena and west down the WI River valley. Return Golf Course Rd. (stopped at APT for info), Amacher Hollow, Roelke etc. Fun! 42 401
28 April This beautiful and challenging ride was laid out by Joe. It is draft 2 of the cancelled Plain pie ride. It put us in North Freedom perhaps 14 minutes before the Railrosd Restaurant was to close. More than one flat would have been bad, though I suspect the 500 ft. climb over the Range on Freedom Rd. would have been easier while not digesting a chicken salad sandwich and apple pie. I have no doubt that the Baraboo Range provides some of the finest landscapes Wisconsin has to offer. This is my pick for the best ride on our schedule. 56 457
1 May It's not that we do not have sufficient great riding on rural roads in Dane and Iowa counties, not to mention Green, Sauk and Richland, still I drove a mere 40 miles to Muscoda, pronounced Mus'codee,which is as northeast as you can be in Grant County. From there, I rode southwest on G, south on Q and Shemak Rd. to way the hell on top of Castle Rock Ridge. AKA Bohemian Ridge. This was against a persistent southerly wind and I think it was 13 miles. From there I had to respond to my need for lunch and took G an estimated 11 miles back to Muscoda. Guys and galls, there is a great Saturday morning ride here: think about it: a mere 45 minutes west of the infamous Shoe Box is Muscoda. From there we ride the 13 miles to Castle Rock. Then it's about 18 miles to the Hometown Cafe in Boscobel where if you misbehave, they will feed you down the street free for up to 99 years! And you get a half hour of excercise every day if you want it. Hope you like concrete. After pie at the Hometown, it couldn't be 16 miles via the GRABRAWR route back to MUScodee. This ride is better than a picnic at the Hyde Tavern an the food prospect is better than at Blanchardville, tho that is one pretty ride. 24 481
5 May Every 18 miles count. Had it been merely wet but warm or cold but dry, this would have been a nice ride--the WNR from Mt Horeb. Dennis flatted on both Wheels at about the same time!! Standing in the cold wind and drizzle put and end to our progress towards New Glarus. I think for the first time in 11 years, I couldn't free my left shoe from the pedal at a critical moment and rolled onto the pavement at zero mph after kicking chain attempting a downshift when I shouldn't have. No significant damage to me or bike, just broken skin below left knee. 18 499
7 May Skys finally cleared by noon leaving clean, refreshing air, tho persistent westerly wind. The way out was via Roelke, Knight Hollow, HH, H, Korback Rd to the ridge of North Clay Hill Rd. An objective was to find out if it remains a gravel road. Well, the south end, the ridge, is paved, but the critical pasrt--the steep hill at the north end where the road drops down to the lower end of Far Look Rd--is gravel. I rode it and it is smooth and well packed, tho I don't intend to lead a ride over it. I sat in the afternoon sun taking-in granola bars and water at Far Look and T. Returned via T, H, Mellum , Ray Hollow, Peterson, Knight Hollow etc. My post ride Dopplebock hits the spot. 37 536
9 May Call this ride Richland Center--Boaz--Bloom City--Rockbridge--Hustlers Ridge--Richland Center. It's about 50 miles, but I added 8 because I did more than that in additional scouting of routes that proved to be gravel. I found this ride 15+ years ago as the Richland Center October Apple Fest ride. Last done with 3 daughters and Mike Hogan this month in '93. Route is west from RC community center (much parking) on Q, Y, Crow Hill School Rd, Q, then north on Jackson (1st I've seen it paved)to Boaz. Be sure to stop at the Historical Marker. Then it's north along Mill Creek on E. Avoid a mile of firm gravel in the valley of Patch Hill Dr. (my eventual route) by taking Lowrey Rd over to A. Take A north past the Ash Ridge Baptist Church, where I finally stopped to lunch, to 56. GO east to Bloom City on I. Don't try any of the many tempting alternatives you will see on maps of this area, unless you like beating your tires on gravel. Go east-southeast to Rockbridge on D. Go south on 80 two miles to left on D. I then chose to take DD up to Hustlers Ridge (called Pleasant Ridge on my map), a devil of a climb. From there it was a constantly rolling ridge ride against the wind to N followed by a nice descent right into the city. I'm nearly dead. 58 594
12 May This exploration of part of the Lower Wisconsin watershed was quite lovely. Roads in this area west and north of Gov. Dodge S. P. are so lightly travelled that the fact that so many town roads are gravel was not awfully disappointing. Even the several miles we rode on WI 130 were "ours" almost alone. The majority of our riding was on county highways. Most memorable a day later was ridge jumping on Limmex Hill Rd between Clyde and the Lower Wyoming Valley. then it was Percussion Rock, Upper Wyoming Valley (my other favorite) and the 360 foot climb on Weaver Rd. Several years since I checked-out this latter region. 46 640
16 May Perfect weather this morning for a good spin. Was well on my way to Dodgeville when I met unrideable loose gravel on Oak Road on its final steep ascent to Pikes Peak Rd. Instead of taking a 7 mile detour to the north to take Pikes Peak Rd up to Dugway where I was originally headed, I took T south east to Barneveld with the idea of taking the trail over to Pikes Peak. Fickle me, I headed south on K from Barneveld instead, went east on H, then south a ways on F enjoying the views. After exploring but not much descending Clay Hill Rd (nice view acoss the valley to Daleyville), having no map, I got back on F retracing a bit. Then on to Mt Horeb for lunch via Barber Rd and E as we did on last years Blanchardville ride. Home via Stewart Park, Bohn Rd, BMT etc. 47 687
17 May Middleton to near Roxbury to X-Plains to Middleton. Nice late afternoon ride with Barb, fresh Pizza at Marias in Glacier Village. Miss those quiet Iowa County roads. 36 723
19 May Another great Saturday ride except for two flats (Adam and me) and two time consuming separations. Mid-afternoon lunch at Deiningers was as good as I could imagine. Though all my choices were excellent, I spent the hour coveting the menu and dessert choices of the other seven at the table. My flat is still a mystery--will check it out tomorrow. 65 788
25 May After 5 solid days off the bike, foxed or spooked by the rainy weather 4 of those 5 days, I took a chance in 55 degree weather but no wind and I rode under often threatening skies to historic Dodgeville for a 1:15 lunch at the Courthouse Inn. I had 70% grease as reward for my effort, perhaps my fault. Climbs on the way out were Roelke, Blue Ridge, Z and the park road from Cox Hollow lake. Hunger ( I didn't leave until 10:15) caused me to shoot the bike lane on 23 rather than loop weszt and south from the Park entrance. While I ate, the peach pie was redeeming, the skies poured on Dodgeville, adding to my feared indigestion and heart attack. I read the State Journal and finally left the courthouse district about 2:40. To my amazement, about a mile north of town, it clearly had not rained! On the return, Idid not ride through the Park. I took 23 to ZZ to Y to H to Roberts to Blue Ridge to Knight Hollow to home by 4:58. Gad, did I luck out! I felt 2 drops of rain the whole 5 hours on the bike. 72 860
26 May 8:45 ride from my place with this year's group except the Blanchard's who would have loved it. Dennis and Dan and I split off from Joe, Pat and Barb at the top of Ryan Rd. Our ride on Mounds View, H, Prairie Rd, Clay Hill Rd, W and 39 with a tailwind was to our liking. We arrived at Brewery Creek Brew Pub 20 some min. ahead of the other group who took a 7 mile down-up route to Barneveld and then the Trail to Y. This Pub remains a fantastic place for lunch: my choices of their IPA, green salad with house dressing, salmon burger and black coffee were excellent: my only regret was passing on the cheesecake or other dessert. We were clobberred by cold showers on the way back, an excellent route through Hyde agreed to by all who know these routes. Winds were very moderate today and temps were in the low fifties, but showers formed in minutes without discipline. I'm glad that we all were prepared for the relative cold and wet, for severe hypothermia would otherwise have created a serious crisis as most times we were miles from help. What a smart and strong group we are! 79 939
28 May Blue Ridge,Barneveld, Mt Horeb Loop from my place with Barb. Light winds from west, 70 degrees and mostly sunny. The details of this route deserve a nicely done que-sheet which I will do. It was 25 miles to Barneveld (Barb's clock), 40 at Mt Horeb and 51 back at my place. Many of the roads on the south slope between Barneveld and Mt Horeb were "first time" for me and they were uniformly lovely. Lunch outside at the Blue Sky was nicely done and served. We each chose Shirley's fish sandwich (beautiful cod and not deep fried! on great bun) and key lime cheesecake (ok, not special). We closed the place down for Memorial Day. With Barb as my critic, I can say this was one grand ride, right Barb? Barb? 51 990
30 May "We ten" minus Barb and Dan rode the WNR from Salmo Pond with minor mods suggested by Joe. One mod was to twist through the Forshaug Valley rather to go around it as the official ride now does. With darkness approaching, I did not do the Barlow Rd detour. I completed the ride except for Scherble? Rd and cruised home arriving at 8:30. 1,000 training miles are showing their value--this was painless! 40 1030
3 June Late afternoon ride--unseasonably cool but no wind. Out FF/KK,K,Mounds Park to BMSP Pool. Back Ryan and BMT. Hills no problem on this short outing. 23 1053
5 June Rode home from Honda Service on Grand Canyon Drive (what a pretentious asphalt name!). Did not get wet. Hate riding in town. Loved the fact that my wonderful bicycle grants me much self-suficiency for most of the year. 18 1071
6 June Airport Rd to Parkwood Plazza in Middleton to order custom bike boxes and pick-up wedding pictures. On to "Grand Canyon Drive" (puke) to pick-up Honda Accord (great car). Love my bike--and my 4-year-old car--whole story. I feel ready to climb in beautiful Colorado. 19 1090
9 June Springdale Luth. Church (near Mt Horeb) to meet others, less Barb, Adam and Dennis, on Fritz Rd. Usual route to New Glarus except Airport Rd--a nice way to approach NG. A good $5.00 lunch from the bakery at 10:30. Except for 1-1/3 miles of gravel on Pioneer Rd, a most pleasant ride back to Mt. Vernon, where I split from the group. 42 1132
