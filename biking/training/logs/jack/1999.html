8 April On Mar 29, I finally lifted my Klein off its hook for the first time since early August, right after returning from a hiking vacation with friends in south central Mountana. Absolutely my only excercise in this duration has been walking between my parking lot and my office. I deeply enjoyed a short ride to Salmo and back after a test ride on Old Indian Trail. This past weekend I walked our dogs mostly on private lands in my neighborhood, and on Apr. 1, I jogged with them to their delight for about a mile. This was my first "run" in about eleven months. I have begun training from a base of near zero and will resist picking up the distances ver rapidly. Reconstructing my homestead, even with mostly excellent contractors, has consumed me beyond expectation and changed my uses of time I used to call recreational. 15 15
10 April It was sunny though windy when we left Dan's at 9:30. What a thrill to finally be "in training" with my like-minded friends. What a thrill to discover that with everyone taking it easy this early-on, I was able to keep up. I felt tired on Sunday and knew it was the effect of the two hour ride on Saturday. >20 15
13 April Evening ride, warm and sunny. South on F, up and back on Ryan Rd and Moyer Rd--simply lovely. Then Blue Mounds Trail (Stamfli's hill) and home to dogs who awaited my return on (their?) new deck. Nice ride, nice return. 15 30
17 April This is one of my favorite Dan Spencer patented rides. That's saying something because it does not go thru a population center other than through a cemetary until almost the end. What it does offer, after you've worked for it, is a ride atop what I may mistakenly call Primrose Ridge. This ridge runs east and west a few miles north of New Glarus. I would guess it's somewhat lower and much shorter than Military Ridge. It probably could not exist if the glaciers had not stopped short of this area. A good time with D, J, E and A. 30 60
24 April Met Dan and Dennis @ 9:30 in Mt Horeb from where we did the Wed night ride under exceptional conditions--still, cool, sparklingly clean air. Also exceptional were two very different close-up sitings of mature red tailed hawks--one perched and one that took flight along side of and then across our paths. In both cases we were riding in a fairly tight group, so we all had about the same experience. Rest of weekend went well. I learned how much more "fun" mechanical extraction of invasives is than plain old weeding. 25 85
29 April Last night's Wednesday night ride--the Riley loop--deserves a DIFFICULT rating for the frequency of meeting up with steep-to-extremely-steep rises. Sure they're short, at least compared to the mountains, but they're tough nevertheless. It was sunny and the air was clear, cool and with a stiff wind from the east. I was happy to come to the end of this enjoyed ride and workout. 20 105
1 May Met J D D E A at Paioli? somewhat after 9:30 for an annual training ride to New Glarus. That's training for Rockies, but also for good food nicely served less than mid-way into an extremely pretty ride. Lunch was at the Tea Room above the Bakery. I had home-made vegetable quiche and others had a potatoe kniche. We all had our choice from their first-rate dessert bar after cleaning our plates. All except for Joe, that is, who did a wise thing--turn around and head back to Madison after breaking a rear spokle on the freewheel side. When that happens, it's no fun going fast. You feel like you're riding on borrowed time. Our route back past Primrose Luth Church put me in a heavenly trance, even though the hills were admittedly taxing--you shouldn't have to pay taxes in heaven. If I were asked to pick the prettiest road of the trip, it would be Spring Rose Rd going east and north from Mt Vernon. The day itself had much to do with my high. Though we did not discuss it, I was sure others experienced it too. 40 145
10 May High pressure returned to Wisconsin May 9 producing perfect cycling weather--light wind, cool air and warm sun. J D D E A & I took full advantage and I certainly put myself to the test. 77 miles is a long way, especially this early in the season. My ride was home to Sauk City. Joined our group there and did the usual route to Plain on Slotty road--oficially rustic--to Natural Bridge S.P., Irish Valley etc. Plain Jane did not know we were coming for lunch, dispaired, and went out of business sometime before we got there. This gave a very nice woman in a new convenience store to look to our refreshment--She promisd Dennis she'd bake us a pie next time if we called her a day ahead! Then it was on to Spring Green for home grown potato leek soup and mineral water for me. Sensibly, Joe guided us back up the river valley to Arena and from there we had only two hills that were monsters to me. With the help of Aleve, I seem to be recovering nicely. 77 222
19 May It's 10 days since my last ride, awfully long for one who is seriously training for "Rockies." Last Saturday, when I scrubbed a ride I was psyched for due to a raw spot where my private parts meet the saddle, Evan was kind enough to urge me to treat it with an anti-fungal, and that has allowed healing to take place. Met dennis and Dan at the foot of Blue Mound and rode much of the Wednesday Night ride with them. Nice evening! Our dogs rewarded my trust in them by apparently behaving themselves perfectly, though they were unrestrained the 2/1-3 hours I was gone. The ride goes past our house, so I get a cyclist's view of what it's like to pass my country home featuring free-range dogs. 26 248
22 May Another Saturday with ideal cycling weather. Road construction caused us to omit the winding and ascending Ryan Rd and the thrilling descent on Mounds Park Rd this time. After Zwetler and Meadow Vale, we hugged the north side of Military Ridge until dumping down into the vally of Mill Creek. After that, due to time constraints, we opted for fairly direct, though lovely, routes to the former The Spring Green (restaurant--sadly no longer) and back to my place. My pace was not to my liking, but heck, I did the hills and the miles and truly enjoyed them. 56 304
26 May Deeply enjoyed this mid-week evening ride from home to Salmo Pond, then the "official" long loop and back to home. 41 miles in just over 3 hours is respectable what with 4 ridges and many rollers. I felt good and feel good after shower and night's sleep. Must admit to trepidation over Saturday's planned ride which I suspect will be the toughest yet. It seems that 40-50 mile rides are about right for me at this time. After that, they begin to feel like 90 mile rides felt a few years ago--that is, like more than sane men choose to do to themselves. 41 345
1 June Dan, Dennis, Joe and I managed to tear ourselves away from Dan and Bonnie's new puppies and ride to Blanchardville and back. It was a lovely ride with Primrose ridge and Lee valley on the way out and surprisingly nice Hwy F on return. It was pretty warm, much warmer than we usually experience in Colorado's high country. A steady and strong tail wind probably lopped an hour off our return and saved the day for me. 57 402
2 June This was (I think) the first time in two or more years that i've done this Wed night ride from Mt Vernon to Dalyville. And a challenging ride it is. Joe and I tried to convince anyone in ear shot that we were sorely disappointed in the flatness of the terrain. Yeh, right. Actually, Dan detoured us to avoid the only truly steep rise, and I rather enjoyed the more moderate angles that did not require my lowest gears. I never cease to be amazed at the runner's high that occurs on these two-hour work outs on the bike. I arrived at the start tired, hungry and irritable. I struggled with the desire to go directly to the comforts of home and refrigerator. I rode and within minutes, I was comfortable and in love with life and those around me. The stupedest thing I could do is to give up this activity while it is still possible for me. 26 428
5 June It's probably just a handfull of times, I'm sure, that I finished a ride more depleted of energy and hydration than I did on this saturday ride with our full contingent plus Dave. The ride to Minreal Point and back started out with favorable temperatures, though with winds from the south/southwest which strengthened until late in the day. But I think it was more the heat and humidity from perhaps 10:00 sun time on that made an already challenging route all the tougher. So I thank the stars that the wind did not die or swing around for the return from MP starting at about 2:00 daylight time. Indeed the wind fairly blew us home, something you non-cyclists should be told is not litterally true. It means that it allows greater speeds for the same energy output and makes climbing noticably easier. Its downside is that it lowers the relative speed at which the rider "breaks" the oncoming air making cooling one's body a big problem when it's hot and humid. I recovered nicely though I could not spare the time to ride on Sunday. 85 513
12 June This ride was the training season finale for me, and a grand one it was. This morning (Monday) I dropped my bike off at Dan Atkins' to be cleaned and boxed for the shuttle from Colorado Springs to Durango. Saturday's was mostly an oncore from Dan's to the New Glarus Tea Room and back to our respective abodes. (I haven't forgotten that our earlier NG ride was to and fro Paoli.) The sky began clearing even before Evan and I arrived at Dan's (Evan arriving by bike) and the three of us enjoyed another beautiful ride. We arrived in an hour and 20 minutes at NG soon after 10:00, just in time for a second breakfast, a fare that settles best just before resuming a ride. I can't get over how little is the traffic and how fine are the views in western Dane County south of Military Ridge, even on county highways like JG. You might guess from my raving reviews that I do not regard "training rides" to be in any substantial way inferior to "real rides" like we will do for seven days straight next week with Ride the Rockies. Big differences in scenery and riding conditions, yes. But for great comradery and personal satisfaction while balancing all our obligations in our busy weeks, these small group "training rides" can't be beat. 42 555
30 June So what was the "real" riding like? Except for short segments of rough road and heavy bike and motorized vehicle traffic, pretty terrific, thank you. Most memorable was the nearly 62 mile climb from Cortez to Lizzard Head Pass, mostly along the Dolores River. Other highlights were rafting on the Arkansas, breakfast in Telluride and Gunnison and supper at Lucky Lady's in Salida. Did mention both sides of Monarch Pass? My 575 training miles were mimimal though sufficient preparation. Is Colorado a better place than Wisconsin for road cycling? Heck no. Take away all the support serevices provided by Ride the Rockies, their sanctioned food vendors and the cadre of highway patrolmen, and much comfort probably would be lost. Colorado has many beautiful vistas, miles upon miles of continuous climbs and descents, plus low humidity. Wisconsin has networks of peaceful, quiet roads, more intimate scenary, as well as many small towns that compensate for RTR's aid stations. (Admittedly CO has these also.) I feel great about the whole effort, not just the 437 miles and thousands of feet of climbing in Colorado. 0 555
