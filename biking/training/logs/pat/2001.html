7 April A nice breezy day for a ride. I got to practice my diagonal biking. I survived even though I had to walk up two hills -- where's the wind at your back when you need it? 26.6 160.7
8 April Out Seminole Hwy.for a recovery ride. The 15mph wind seemed like nothing after yesterday's ride! It was a nice ride, sunny and the air was dry and cool and felt refreshing. 13.5 174.2
11 April Well, it was not the 30-40mph winds w/gust to 60 of Saturday, but it was still windy at 20-30mph gusting to 40 -- I still did a little tilt-riding. Joe & I did the wnr and only 3 others showed up! (vs. the 40 or so at last week's wnr). We took a shortcut and so did a shorter route than normal for the Bellville ride. Though it was rainy and windy when we left Madison, we went anyway following Joe's rain rules -- and what do you know? It was clear for our ride and we had some really beautiful light on the landscapes. I'm glad I went. 13.7 187.9
13 April Out Seminole Hwy and thereabouts from home. What a beautiful morning with fresh, cool, dry air -- and though we had a head wind heading home, it was not all that much. It was my first time on the new Time ATAC pedals and I didn't fall down or anything. The hills seemed easier, but I'll see how it goes on our rtr ride Saturday. At PD we ran into 3 other bikers, one of whom recognized Joe by his wheels -- turns out he built them at ChronoMetro! It was fun, because when the light changed and we headed down the hill, I lead the way in high gear. They then, of course, passed us going up to the overpass -- as was expected! 16.5 204.4
14 April A great ride to New Glarus for lunch; the air was what I liked most -- fresh, dry and cool. The views were wonderful and spring was in the air. And despite the fact that we did more hills than last Saturday, I didn't walk up any.... This was my first long ride in clipless pedals and I think they helped a lot with the hills. I enjoyed everyone's company. 36.4 240.8
15 April Home out Seminole Hwy. ride w/Grandview to Fitchrona to Lacy loop; 50's partly sunny; a nice ride after the longer rtr ride of yesterday; also, we had a nice brunch at Bluephies on the way back home! Also, Joe & I did a couple of hill experiments and I have to conclude that, though I coast faster than he does initially, he accelerates during the coast faster than I -- he beat me on both downhill coasting trials. 16.4 257.2
20 April My longest ride ever! Joe mapped us out a real nice route -- basically the Lodi ride that rtr group will do tomorrow, but starting/stopping our house in Madison and going to Fish and Crystal lakes; it was a treat to have actual pedalling miles for a change -- that is, we had hill but also a lot of relatively flat good pedalling miles; hundreds of Coots on Crystal Lake plus a beautiful Kingfisher on a line by the road; lunch at Lodi at the cafe restaurant across from "Home of Susie" the duck; mostly just glad I feel this good after the ride; the worst hill was on Enchanted Valley Road, and I'm wondering if there is a meaning to "enchanted" I was previously unaware of! Hope I do OK a week from Sunday, when I will have been off my bike for 8 days straight in FLA taking courses. 59.0 316.2
29 April A fifty mile ride turned into 68, needless to say I was not pleased -- especially given the fact that I was off the bike for the past eight days. The thirty-two mile ride to Mt. Horeb had some hills and head wind and I naively thought the wind would work to our favor on the way back. However, I neglected to take into account "Pat's Rule." Which states, "tho wilt always have a headwind." The return to Madison went reasonably well during the first nine miles thereafter, I went into a bit of decline -- culminating in a death march in the last fifteen miles from Cross Plains. But I survived and saw blue birds, gold finches, herd cranes, saw large patches of marsh marigolds, virginia marigolds, newly leafing trees, so all in all it wasn't too bad. Also I recommend the Blue Sky Cafe in Mt. Horeb a few doors down from Schuberts. We had nice breakfast there. 68.2 384.4
1 May Out Seminole and back to work out the kinks. Tried but was unable to shake Joe on the downhill today. He was on me like glue. 13.4 397.8
2 May The Festge Park Wed. Night Ride. Threatening thunder when we arrived, but only got some sprinkles. Otherwise, a wonderful air and great smell from the little bit of rain. What beautiful countryside and views. I enjoyed going up Table Bluff near the end of the ride bec. I was alone at the time and could enjoy looking around and the bird sounds. I met Joe who waited for me at the top and when we got to the Festge Park parking lot I was surprised that we were there already! I thought there would be more hills yet. A very pleasant surprise. 23.1 420.9
5 May RTR ride from Grundahl Park, Mt. Horeb; rainy, windy and cold (40's); group decision to turn back after about the first ten miles; my legs felt like lead today; Dennis got TWO flats; Jack took a fall; all and all the ride could have been a lot more pleasant!; warm-up (as much as possible when all your clothes/shoes are soaked through) and lunch at Blue Sky Cafe w/Barbara, Joe, Dennis & Dan; Jack, Wendy & David opted to head home to hot showers straight away -- wise move. 20 440.9
6 May Joe designed us a nice ride in Iowa co. where we saw no bad cloud cover in the radar (in an attempt o avoid the rain of yesterday); we began at Gov. Dodge, down the most beautiful road -- Norwegian Hollow Road --- over the ridge on PP, down into the valley on sand hill road, and we were in the WI River valley; there across the river into Sauk Co., worst ten miles going east, flat and into the wind to Spring Green for lunch; after that first 40 miles a final 15 back to Dodge; return was pretty, though I was pretty tired. Saw spring phlox, woodland geraniums, solomon's seal, but no morels... goldfinches, blue birds, a wacked out heron apparently gliding on the wind (but like a kite caught in gusts), heard cranes ... nice weather, 50's to 60ish, cloudy, wind out of the east. 56.3 497.2
11 May Morning out Seminole Hwy; ave. 14.9 mi/hr 13.9 511.1
12 May Started out the ride with the rtr group from Gov. Dodge, similar to last week's ride with Joe. It was cool (50's) and overcast. However, after the first steep bit at the end of Norwegian Hollow Road, my head exploded in pain. I'd never felt anything like it -- it came on so suddenly and severely. It felt like all the capillaries in the top part of my brain suddenly burst open releasing some pain-causing agent. I just stood there holding my head for quite a while. It was rather frightening. Luckily Joe was there too and could ride ahead to tell the others we were going to head back instead of continuing with the ride. After standing around a bit, it subsided slightly and Joe and I headed back Norwegian Hollow taking our time. It was actually enjoyable to see that lovely road at a slow pace. When we were standing by the side of the road further on, someone stopped to ask if we needed help which I thouht was real nice. However, I'm glad that we didn't ask for a ride back to Gov. Dodge, as I think that the leisurely ride back Norwegian Hollow may have helped my head -- I also sucked on a hard candy and I think the sugar to my brain also helped. Sorry to have missed the ride with the others, but I think that Joe and I did the right thing. 9.1 520.2
13 May Out from home to Oregon -- Joe designed us a great route, beautiful, plus on Storeytown Road we were treated to a great Mother's Day sighting of two Sandhill Cranes and their two little ones! We watched for quite a while as they moved about in a field and got to see them very well. Lunch in Oregon at Nellie's Bronx Cafe -- nice vegi burger and fries. I felt good during and after this 50 miler and that was a good feeling after my head problem of yesterday. Sunny and beautiful 60's (67 at lunch). 50.5 570.7
14 May Morning easy ride out Seminole to work out some of the lactic acid buildup in my hamstrings from yesterday. 13.7 584.4
15 May Morning ride out Seminole Hwy; uneventful except for the baboon we encountered at intersection of Seminole and PD -- while waiting in the lane for going straight or turning right, the baboon passed within 6 inches of Joe on his left, then turned right in front of us; after Joe tapped the baboon's van in protest, the baboon backed up, got out and puffed up his chest in Joe's face, shouting ensued and he finally got back in his van and drove off, evidently not believing us when we assured him of the rules of the road for bicyclists and motorists.........too many idiots in the world. 13.6 598
16 May Did the WNR from the other Blue Mound, Brigham Park. Most of the group was there except for Barbara and Jack (though we saw Jack en route when we passed his house -- he had done a 47 miler that am). After the 3.6 mile downhill start, the rest of the group soon went on without us (Joe stayed with me), and I think they did the longer ride because they werenn't back when we were. The scenery was beautiful with Columbine like crazy, and woodland geraniums, meadow rue, maidenhair ferns, sweet rocket............It was a hilly ride, but my newer technique of slowing down my cadence helped me a lot to go up them without too much trouble. My head hurt a bit, but not like last weekend's episode. It was warm and humid and Wisconsin's state bird, the mosquito, was out in great numbers. The sunset was gorgeous as we headed home. 23.1 621.1
19 May Ride from our house to Poynette and back; a ride through mostly open farm country pretty much due north of town; clear skies and mid-70's, light winds; we left about 9am, so it was not too hot by our return at about 3pm; we took it easy, stopped for a snack at Dane (both ways) and had lunch at the Main St. Merchant's Walk Restaurant in Poynette, where we were waited on by one of the owners (who had never waited before) because the place was hopping and they were short-staffed; passed a really neat stone house with stone outbuildings just north of Dane -- it looked like it could have been in the french countryside; feel pretty good after the ride and am hoping I feel the same way after a 50-miler tomorrow. 62.6 683.7
20 May Repeat of our Oregon ride of last weekend; nice day 70's, winds sse 10-15; the sweet smell of the flowering locust trees everywhere, plus the sweet rocket; 111 miles between yesterday's and today's rides -- hopefully this will help get me through next weekend's 80 miler to Mineral Point. 49.1 732.8
23 May Did the new WNR from Indian Lake to Mazomanie; the cloud formations made for spectacular and dramatic skies; we got rained on a bit near Mazo, but otherwise it was dry and cool -- in fact I was a bit chilled being underdressed with only my bike shorts a long sleeve top and wind breaker. Luckily I also had my rain mittens to wear. It must have been in the 40's. Beautiful ride though. The spiderwort is starting to bloom; I thought that Dunlop Hollow Rd. was really pretty with nice stone houses here and there in addition to the natural stone foundations. Didn't see anyone else from RTR group there. Was fun talking to Levi Wood at the beginning. 26.8 759.6
26 May My longest ride ever! The Mineral Point ride with the RTR group minus the Blanchards. I had a good ride. Cool weather -- 50's, dramatic skies, got soaked somewhere between 40 & 50 miles after lunch at Mineral Point. Otherwise I enjoyed the beautiful ride -- flowers galore and a deer that was frozen on a hillside in the woods about 30 feet away and looking back at us as we went up a hill -- what a nice sighting. However, because we got wet, I have butt sores (another first! Joe now feels I'm fully initiated in the true bikies experience.) -- I think it was from the chafing of my wet bike shorts -- the affect may have been compounded by the leg warmers pulled up underneath the leg portions of my shorts which may have made the shorts move against my skin a bit more than usual. No problem while on the ride, just later. 78.7 838.3
30 May The Salmo Pond WNR w/most of the rtr group (minus Dan, Barb) -- what a great ride! The air was fresh and cool (around low 60's?), everyone in the group seemed to be feeling good and smiling alot! Lots of hills and they didn't seem hard to me --> I'm finally getting in shape, I guess! I particularly enjoyed Forschaug Rd. -- a wall to begin, then a nice downhill, then another wall, then down to the end; Barlowe was great -- never went down such a long and nice downhill. My only regret on those downhills was braking so much -- I think my max on Barlowe was 48, but could have easily been in the 50's; the mood of the group was a good one and I think we all are getting excited to hit the rockies! I can't wait. The Salmo pond ride is a challenging hill ride, but I felt great at the end and could have done another!!!! Who would have thought? 26.1 864.4
3 June Aiming for a long, less hilly ride, joe plotted us a course to Cambridge for lunch via Stoughton (where we had coffee and donuts); nice lunch at the Old Mill restaurant in Cambridge; because it was 40 miles at that point, we went back via McFarland (rather than the route south of Stoughton that Joe had originally planned which would have been more like 50 miles); cool day, felt like October -- but at least no rain and less wind than yesterday when we had originally planned to ride. All in all a good ride, though a lot less beautiful and pleasant than most of the rides west of Madison. My last long ride before Rockies! probably we'll do a 40-50 miler next weekend before we go. 80.1 944.5
6 June WNR - Lodi. As usual, I liked the Lodi ride. It was a drizzly/foggy evening, but Joe and I had a fun bike ride. Evan and Adam showed -- I didn't see anyone else from the rtr group there. 26.8 971.3
9 June I rolled over my first 1,000 miles today, before I turn my first 48 years tommorrow! Nice, though longer than expected, ride with the rtr group (minus Adam, Dennis, Barb) to New Glarus -- the problem was that it was about 16 there, but 28 back --> we all pretty much ran out of water and had to refill at Mt Vernon. Hope that water was OK. Anyway, it's off to rockies on Wed. I guess I'm as ready as I'll be! 44.6 1015.9
