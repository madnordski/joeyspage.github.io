3 March Evan and Adam Cross Plains and back from home. Beautiful,sunny day - 36° First time this year it felt like a "real ride"! 20.2 66.2
4 March Old Sauk-Cross Plains and back via Airport/Cleveland. Sunny, but colder with 10-20 NW wind. The Rensho with King Mario tires seemed to climb better than the Klein today! 23.1 89.3
17 March Adam & Evan. To Cross Plains and back via Airport Rd. and Cleveland Hill, adding a climb of the road extending stagecoach across Cty.S. The weather is really encouraging this early season riding - we're getting our money's worth out of that cold weather riding gear! Determined that Adam's DeRosa still fits him well, without adjustment. And we both believe that it's ride quality exceeds all our other bikes to date, despite it's extra weight (it hung on the TREK store scale at 20.4lbs.)! 23.5 112.8
18 March Cross Plains and back via Old Sauk. This was a beautiful, sunny 45-50° day,and for the first time, no booties were needed! Wendy took her inaugural ride on her new Titanium Mongoose and fell in love! This was also her first experience with STI (anything not down-tube) shifting and clipless pedals - she advanced about 20 years in technology in one day and couldn't believe the improvement! David rode my Klein, about 3" too long, but was very strong and rode the front with Adam. His bike arrives tomorrow (unknown to him) for his birthday next Saturday. Thus starts what will probably be the most memorable bicycling summer of my life.... 20.4 133.2
20 March Adam & I rode quickly out to Cleveland Hill, averaging 17.8 on the way. Adam hit 52.1 mph down Cleveland (Evan 50).We continued up Deer Run and back home, averaging 16.0 overall, climbing a total of 870 ft. My legs were dead after this less than 1 hour hard ride. Adam's weren't, but you know how those teenagers lie... 15 148.2
22 March David melded with his new bike today! After he reluctantly agreed to another ride on my too-long (for him) Klein, we surprised him with the Trek OCLV - it fit perfectly and worked flawlessly - he had a great ride and hasn't stopped thanking us! Went down Cleveland and back, via Blackhawk. Avg.15mph. 920 ft. climbed. 15 163.2
24 March Adam and I took a short spin to Stagecoach and back - very cold (23°) with a 10-20mph NW wind.Avg.15.5 mph. 670 ft. climbed. Wendy "spun" at Supreme, where she's getting plenty of early "miles". 15.1 178.3
29 March Everybody was busy, so I (Evan) went alone, home to Airport, Schneider, Enchanted Valley, Hilltop and back. Cool at 48°, cloudy and spitting rain at times, with a moderate East wind. Avg.16.3. Climbed 1470ft. 25 203.3
31 March Having missed the AM Saturday Ride, David and I set out for Observatory/Barlow on a nice 56-58° day, lightly clad. Heading West on Mineral Point, all of a sudden all h_ll broke loose, with wind blowing us sideways, whipping driven rain like needles in our face, and the temp dropped some 15-20°! We turned around and rode freezing and soaking wet (water squishing in our shoes with every pedal stroke) in a heavy rain and got home unable to feel our fingertips - like dead wood when using the brake/shift levers. Averaged 15.5. The coldest I've ever been on a bicycle. This was David's second ride on his new bike - he never complained! 15.8 219.1
1 April Adam and Evan went through the commercial park to Airport, Cap. View to Schneider, Enchanted Valley to Hilltop and back via Rocky Dell, Cleveland and Old Sauk. 48° and sunny on what was supposed to be a miserable day! Avg.15.3mph. Climbed 1610ft."Measured" (as best we could) the grade on Hilltop as 13.3% at it's steepest and 6% overall. 27.3 246.4
3 April Evan alone.Home to Stagecoach via Old Sauk, then south to Observatory and Barlow to Min. Pt. and back by the same route, Stagecoach and Old Sauk trail. 1620ft. climbed. Avg.16.6. Sunny skies, 45° and an unusual East wind, 10-15mph. 29.5 275.9
7 April I didn't think I could top the coldest wet ride of my life one week ago, but Windy Ridge today was more memorable. We had numerous forced forays into the fields, careening crabapples and chainring-cut calves, and were lucky no one was hurt more seriously. Gusts were 60mph! Climbed 1730ft. 27.1 303
8 April A nice, sunny 60° day for class - Climbing 202, taught by Adam on our "Circle of Hills". We went from home out Old Sauk to Stagecoach, south on P to Observatory, over the hill and up Barlow. Turned around and went down Barlow(52mph!) - we met Dan heading up at the base - back up West Observatory, North on P, side trip up/down "Stagecoach Hill," on to Hillpoint, down Enchanted Valley to Airport, Rocky Dell to Cleveland, side trip up/down Deer Run, up Cleveland hill and back via Blackhawk. Climbed 3140ft. Avg.13.9mph. I have a pretty good impression of Adam's rear end to remember this ride... 44.3 347.3
13 April Beautiful sunny day - 60° with light winds. Adam and I took David on his hardest ride to date. From home out Old Sauk to Stagecoach, South on P and over Observatory to climb up Barlow. Turned around and came down Barlow (53.2mph) to Garfoot, KP and Cross Plains, then home by Airport and Cleveland to Old Sauk. Climbed 1950ft. Avg. 14mph. David did surprisingly well (his total mileage is now just 90), but was out of gas at the end. 33.6 380.9
14 April Beautiful day and ride! These tree-lined, meandering roads we rode are some of my favorites, and I love the rises and falls from the vistas to the river valleys. Climbed 2090ft. on my altimeter, but nearly 3000 by Joe's, raising the question of how elevation gain is and should be measured. I hope _mine_ is wrong! Avg. about 14mph. Nice lunch at Stubbe's in New Glarus. 38 418.9
19 April The whole family struck out for an early evening ride, aware we'll miss our usual weekend miles. Out Old Sauk, down Cleveland and on to Airport via Rocky Dell, N. on Enchanted Valley to P and the Hillpoint loop, back down Enchanted Valley, Airport to Everglade to home. Beautiful cool evening. Climbed 1650ft. Avg. 13.8mph. 28.2 447.1
21 April Strong, gusty winds reminded me of Alamosa. The dramatic, nonsteady directional difference in noise and effort really tried my patience! With everyone at school/church functions I headed out with my beeper and threw caution to the wind, so to speak. Out Old Sauk to Stagecoach, P, Observatory, Barlow, back down(53mph!), Garfoot, KP, P to the full Hillpoint loop to P, Enchanted Vly, Airport, Rocky Dell, Cleveland and home via Blackhawk. Climbed 2350ft. Avg. 15.5mph. 40.3 487.4
24 April Quick spinner out Old Sauk to Timber Lane and down and back up Old Sauk Trail, then down/up Cleveland, using new cyclometer/inclinometer/altimeter(!) showing a 17% grade on Cleveland. Avg.16.1. 950 ft climbed. Adam went 23 miles, to Cross Plains and back via Airport/Cleveland, averaging 19.1mph, arriving home just as I was leaving, so he turned around and joined me! His total was 38 miles, avg. 17.75mph. 15 502.4
25 April Wednesday night ride - Riley loop. Adam and I. Rode with the lead pack for 7-8 miles, but dropped mildly on a series of hills. 60°. Perfect weather. Avg.18mph. Climbed 1450ft. 22.8 525.2
26 April Home to the "three hills" - Old Sauk Trail, Cleveland and Deer Run - and back.Adam & Evan. SW wind 10-20. Avg. 15, climbed 1150ft. 16 541.2
28 April Fabulous Freedom Foray! Thanks to Joe for an interesting route with a memorable S-curve "wall" on Hoot-Owl(?) road. This was rural Wisconsin at it's finest, with beautiful Spring weather. Dan did his part cleaning up the trash when his tire removed a metal sliver from the road. David amazed me in tolerating 4hrs 20mins of riding time this early in his first riding season, and he's not even sore this morning! This day, with approx. 3450 ft. of climbing, approaches some RTR days - I think everyone will be all right this year! The rest of the family's totals thus far: Adam 520, Wendy 194, David 236. However, Wendy completed the "Tour de Marche" at Supreme, and now totals 15 Spinning sessions! 57.8 599
2 May Festge Park WNR. Met Joe, Pat and Dan. Cool, cloudy and almost windless. Fantastic roads here! Every ride should feel this way. 1480 ft. Avg. 15. 22.9 621.9
3 May Evening jaunt to Cleveland and Deer Run hills with the family. The heavy rain started as we pulled into the driveway. 13.4 635.3
6 May Adam & I rode to Indian Lake (to revisit the site of his Eagle Scout project last year) via Everglade, Capitol View, Schneider, Vosen, K, Martinsville, Whipporwill and back on K, Indian Trail, Martinsville, Enchanted Valley, side trip down West Hillpoint, Airport, Rocky Dell, Cleveland, Old Sauk. Climbed 2420ft. Avg. 16. We "tested" the packaged Rice Krispie Treats, which tasted artificial (we'll make our own next time), and Gatorade bars, both peanut butter (good) and chocolate(better), which are still fairly dense but preferable to Powerbars or Cliff Bars, for us. I think carrying a large Fried Cinnamon Roll in a baggie, warmed in the jersey pocket, sounds more appealing! 39.3 674.6
9 May WNR from Oregon. (Rode a little extra at the end). The whole family went - met and rode with Dennis and Jack.Easy, enjoyable and eye-pleasing. 22.9 697.5
10 May Evan alone. To Cross Plains and back the usual way, with side trip to top of Deer Run and back up Cleveland. Climbed 1100ft. Avg.16.2 22.8 720.3
12 May Beautiful circle from Gov. Dodge through Iowa County on a beautiful day. Due to uncertainty about the route and advice from locals, we cut the ride to Avoca somewhat, following N in. Missed Clyde road on the way back, but Limmox Hill, Upper Wyoming and Weaver were spectacular! Avg.14. 2700ft. climbed. Ate at the Country Inn in Avoca - quite good burgers and fries, though the smoke was hard on Wendy's eyes. It was sad to lose Joe & Pat due to headache, but at least she's OK. We left about half of this ride undone - something to consider in the future. 46.8 767.1
13 May Adam, Evan and David started from Black Earth, to Ryan Road via Blue Mounds Trail, on to the top of Blue Mounds and back by Blue Mounds Road, K, Zwettler, F, Reeve, Fosenfeld, KP. I measured 900 ft. from the start of Ryan Road to the top of Blue Mounds, verifying Joe's reading, allowing for slight variation. Avg. 14.2. 2270ft. climbed. 31.2 798.3
16 May WNR - Brigham Park. With Joe, Pat, Dan, Dennis. Mosquitos are out! We couldn't wait at some of the corners as some in the group were being devoured and didn't have enough blood to spare! This is a great route. Descent on Ryan is finest this year! Rode back to a fabulous sunset view. Climbed 2300 ft.(Mounds park felt like all of that!) - 81 ft./mile, the most for any ride this year. Avg. humdrum 14. 28.2 826.5
19 May RTR Group, minus Joe&Pat. Dan's to New Glarus - Lunch at 2:30 at Deninger's was fabulous! Lost Barb early at Fritz/Jimtown, but Jack found her at Fritz/69. Adam flatted and half went ahead but followed York Center while the trailers went on to H (from Hay Hollow). So we didn't meet in Postville as planned, and the arrival/lunch in New Glarus was delayed further. The day was warm (80-85°) and sunny and the terrain was, as usual, gorgeous. Jack flatted near the end. This was a great RTR prep, with good length and 3870 feet climbed (on the Speedzone altimeter). 66 892.5
20 May The family went out to climb some hills. Home to Pine Bluff and up Observatory (16%) to get to Barlow (sustained 18%), then back down (Adam hit 55.6mph) and up Observatory(16%) to P, on to Stagecoach Hill(18%) and Culvers in Cross Plains for late Lunch. Weather cut ride short, so returned the usual route rather than Hillpoint/Ench.Vly/Airprt/Clevlnd. 31.2 923.7
24 May Adam and Evan - quick after supper ride to Stagecoach Hill, up and down and back. Avg 17.2. 19 942.7
26 May Morning ride prior to leaving for Minn. We all went out to the top of Hillpoint and back through Cross Plains. Nice, cool, cloudy morning - everyone felt great! Avg. 14. Climbed 1800 ft. Individual Totals to date: Evan - 971 Adam - 950 David- 488 Wendy- 449(plus ~18 Spinning classes!) 28.7 971.4
28 May Blanchard family with Joe, whose route finding was invaluable in making this such a great day! Sorry to have missed the M. P. ride, we tried to construct a suitable replacement, refining as we rode. Left our home, to Lodi via Capitol View, Church and Pahl roads, made a side trip up/down Gibraltar Rock (can you say steeeeeep?) en route to the Merrimac Ferry, Lunch at Schoolhouse Restuarant, back on Marsh to the Ferry, on to the Wollersheim Winery by E. Harmon and Gastrow, back via Inama, Hornung, Fish Lake, Crystal Lake and Lee road all the way to DM, City of Dane, Hyslop, Wipperfurth, Kingsley, Pheasant Branch and High Point. Rest stops in Lodi, the Winery and Dane were perfectly spaced. Fabulous day, and we all gained a lot of confidence from the 6 hrs 20 min in the saddle - no one was hurting! Of course, this is all "old hat" to Joe by now - he rode to and from the house, adding 11 miles! A Porch Pils afterwards capped a memorable ride, which I will certainly repeat in the future! Avg. 13.8, 3350 ft. climbed. 84.5 1055.9
30 May WNR - Salmo Pond Loop, with RTR group minus Dan and Barb. Detours to Forschaug (I love this road!) and down Barlow (Adam 55.4, Evan 54.1, David 49). Another great riding evening, excepting the bugs! Avg. 14.3. Climbed 1950 ft, for an impressive 74 ft. per mile.Everyone looked strong. 26.2 1082.1
3 June 50° and 10-15mph SW wind. Due to Adam's Eagle Court of Honor and early cold, Adam, David and Evan simply went out to do Stagecoach hill and back by the usual route. Adam's rear flatted on way out. Everyone climbed strongly today. Adam beat his old best up Old Sauk Trail by 8 seconds.Avg. 15.8. 940 ft. climbed (on old Avocet altimeter, which seems to record about 20% less than the new Specialized). This is the _first_ weekend we can say that weather made a significant impact on our mileage, which should have been 60-80 miles total. What a great season we've had! 18.5 1100.6
6 June Lodi MNR with Joe and Pat. Cloudy, cool, with slight mist on Marsh road. Seemed like a sunny day, however, when Pat brought out her leopard skin (pattern) arm warmers! 26.7 1127.3
9 June Paoli-New Glarus with Dan, Joe, Pat and Jack. 85° and sunny.Nice and easy! 44 1171.3
11 June Last ride before RTR! To Cross Plains and back via Hillpoint, Ench. Vly, Airport, Cleveland (sustained 16%), ) Old Sauk. David was tired. Evan with new cleats and Bottom Bracket (not creaking after Teflon tape revision!). Now my front der. cable is stretching! David on new seat; final adjustments made. New brake pads all around. Adam on new Bar/Stem. There always seems to be a lot to do with maintenance around this time! Individual final totals: Evan 1200.1 Adam 1154.1 David 690.2 Wendy 632.4, plus spinning twice a week, regularly! 28.7 1200
