Bicycling Community Page Training Log, 2000
by Joseph King and Scott Rose

Barbara Lukes

Added on Fri Mar 17 07:50:45 2000

DateDescriptionMiles Total
26 February Biked through Shorewood to Dairy Queen for ice cream. 10 10
27 February Rode to Verona with Jim and Angie for breakfast at the truck stop. The french toast and hashbrowns tasted especially good, probably because it was the first breakfast of the season. Jim took us back a different way to avoid having to go up "the hill". But no more of that. I have to start looking for hills. 27 37
23 March Biked to the cemetery on Kingsley Rd. and back. 12 49
25 March Jim and I biked to Cross Plains for breakfast. Windy, gusts up to 32 mph and drafting behind him doesn't work since he's on a recumbant. But we got our reward. Coming back on Schneider Road, we were moving along at 40 mph. It was tricky after we got on hwy 12, side gusts wanted to blow us into the traffic lane. 23 72
26 March Jack and I biked to Lodi. It started to rain as we were getting ready to leave. We debated about 1 minute and decided to head out anyway thinking we probably would not get as far as Lodi. But we did. The rain stopped shortly after we left and held off until we just got back to Middleton. We had a nice tail wind going out which I wasn't aware of until Jack clued me in. So it was easy and fun going out, good lunch in Lodi and back by way of Black Hill Rd, which I didn't think was so bad after dire warnings from Jack. But then the winds picked up and we headed into them all the way back to Middleton. By the time we got to the last hill which isn't that much, I had to use my granny gear. Good ride! 40 112
31 March Just around Middleton 7 119
1 April First ride with the RTR group. Headed out to Mt. Vernon from near Verona. Evan said we climbed 1200 feet. I like to hear what I've accomplished. The group nicely waited for me twice. I do okay on the flat but start to lose it going up the hills. 22 141
2 April Picked up Jim and Angie and headed to Cross Plains. Met up with Jack on Stagecoach and Betta in Cross Plains. Then to Black Earth and the Lunch Bucket for the best pancakes around. After breakfast, Betta headed back to Ashton and Jim, Angie, Jack and I continued on to Mazo, over to Roxbury and back by way of K and Enchanted Valley. We were dying by then. Winds picked up and we started getting hungry and nothing in our bags to eat. By the time we got back to my place, I had to give Angie some orange juice and cookies so she could make it home. Jim didn't go into work today because his back was sore. We parted with Jack at Schuman road and betting that he did just fine and probably felt great when he got home. 51 192
5 April Headed towards Waunakee and back on my new clipless pedals! 13 205
6 April Rode to Trek to have them re-mount my computer. Couldn't get done, because they didn't have a part, so they special ordered it. 10 215
9 April From Middleton, Jack and I headed out for Mt. Horeb. When we got to the Riley Tavern we stopped for a short break. Jack didn't really want to stop since we had only biked 11 miles and I didn't need the break, I just like the Riley Tavern. So we were there about 15 minutes and then on to Mt. Horeb where we had lunch. Spent more time there than we wanted to, service and the kitchen were slow. Then out JJ (or GG) near Little Norway for the prettiest part of the ride, past Tyrol and towards Black Earth and a stop at Jack's place. A couple more hills and then back in Middleton. A good challening ride and one of the prettiest. 47 262
13 April Out to Meffert to hwy 12 back to Kingsley to Woodland and back to Middleton. 15 277
15 April A hilly ride to New Glarus with the RTR group. I was more tired afterwards than I think I should have been after only 35 miles. I'm a litle worried about that. 35 312
18 April Rode to Delatalia for supper with Jim and Angie. 10 322
22 April A nice ride to Lodi from Festge Park with the RTR Group. One of the highlights of the ride for the others, not me, was a porta-potti sitting on a pontoon boat at Crystal Lake. Felt good when I got back. 46 368
24 April It was not a good ride to Waunakee. Jim and I headed into the wind all the way, with maybe the exception of 1/4 mile. It was better going back, but still not good. 17 385
25 April A good ride to Verona with Jack. Light winds,perfect termperatures, and a nice meal at the truck stop, topped off by a half piece of fresh strawberry pie. 26 411
30 April It was a perfect day for the Minnesota Ironman. Temperature in the 70's and windy. But that was okay, it was good for my training. 5500 riders. We started in Lakeville and rode south to Northfield. Mostly rolling hills. I was with Jim, Angie and Linda. Linda cut out at 42 miles. We didn't finish til 5, mostly because Angie and I got waylaid at the St. Olaf College bookstore buying t-shirts. After a quick shower at Karen and Pat's, we hit the road, stopped for a bite to eat and got home at 12:30. 65 476
2 May An after work ride out to the end of Woodland Road. 16 492
3 May A good work out with the Wednesday night ride. We started from Festge Park and headed out toward Martinsville and around. Unfortunately, most of the time my head was to the pavement, so I missed alot of the prettiest of the ride, but I did smell the flowers! The crab trees are at their peak and I did see a grove of them on my left going up Table Bluff Road. Felt good when I got home. Maybe the Wednesday night rides aren't so bad after all. 23 515
4 May Met Betta at Vosen and Schneider Roads. Rode in to Cross Plains for supper. As usual, there was the wind, always the wind. 19 534
6 May Jack and I left Mt. Horeb about an hour earlier than the others, did an extra loop of about 8 or 9 miles, and arrived in New Glarus at exactly the same time as Evan and Adam, and Dan and Dennis, both of whom also left at different times. Had a nice lunch at Deningers, sitting on the porch. It was a good ride, alot of hills. I started to feel comfortable, and actually enjoy, going down the steeper ones. Hope the good feeling lasts. We climbed about 2700 feet. 48 582
7 May Rode to Waunakee for breakfast. 23 605
10 May A good workout with Jack. From Middleton to Cross Plains, looking for hills, back by way of Hilltop, a new road for me, Enchanted Valley, Schneider. Final decision made tonight. I'm going! 25 630
14 May Jack and I met in Riley for 20 miles of some hills and alot of wind. 20 650
15 May Out Woodland to Lodi-Springfield Rd and back. 17 667
16 May Met Betta and rode to Pott's Inn for supper. Great horse fries! 17 684
20 May It was a good training ride, but not a good ride for me. My legs just didn't want to work. I was pretty tired when I got home, but recovered within a couple of hours, so that was hopeful. About all I remember of the ride, is a break at a cemetery, lunch in Hollandale and a lot of signs that said Primrose Lutheran Church. 64 748
21 May The ride to Stoughton wasn't the piece of cake I thought it would be. Probably, because I was a little tired from yesterday, and the wind issue. It was John, Norm, Jim, Brian and I who met at Olbrich for the Bombay Styendai Mai ride. Temperatures were cool,skies overcast and there is always the wind on this ride. We had lunch in Cambridge at the bakery and then out again and really having to buck those winds. We made a short stop in Utica at the general store and still got into Stoughton before the parade ended. Watched it for 5 minutes and then headed over to the Creamery for ice cream. We got our tail wind leaving Stoughton and almost all the way back to Madison. It helped my disposition greatly and I felt good when I got home. 64 812
23 May Nice ride with Jim and Angie to Pott's Inn for supper. 20 832
25 May Nice ride out Meffert, Woodland and into Waunakee for a bite to eat. 28 860
28 May I really didn't want to do this training ride to Mineral Point, after what was not a very good ride the week before. But I did it and got some of my confidence back. I felt good riding and felt good when I got home. 79 939
29 May I'm finding biking is really a state of mind. I biked so much better yesterday than today, and it wasn't because I was tired. I was with Jim, Angie, John and Norm and I had no pressures so that's how I biked. As a result, you'd never would have known I have twice the training miles in that they have, but I didn't really bike any better. But it was a nice ride to Lodi, lunch topped off by a piece of rhubarb custard pie. I know how to live, yesterday it was coconut macroon pie. Back home in time to get to a picnic. Fun Memorial Day! 39 978
3 June A nice comfortable last group training ride to Evansville from Paoli. I might actually miss these Saturday rides. The guys are a nice group to bike with, waiting for me at turns so I wouldn't veer off course. 48 1026
6 June At 2:00 Jack and I left his place for a ride to Dodgeville. A couple of miles into the ride, I tried to shift going up a hill. It didn't work and I fell off my bike again trying to get my foot out of the pedal. After that, I was spooked the whole rest of the ride. I rode alot of it with only one foot clipped in. Alot of hills going, more than I have ever done on a ride. Had Chinese for supper and left Dodgeville around 6:30. We then had the pressure of getting back before night fall. It was an easy route going back, some fun slingshots. But then we got to Blue Mounds Trail. Keep me away from there. I made a wrong turn again. By that time, it was fairly dark, I didn't know where I was, so I ended up stopping at a house and a nice man by the name of Dave took me to Jack's in his truck. I had misunderstood Jack's instructions on where to turn, so I turned too soon. Got home around 10. I was very happy to get there. 68 1094
10 June With Jim, Angie, Betta, Cindy and Mary Kay, rode to Mazo with a stop in Black Earth for breakfast. New cook at the Lunch Bucket, pancakes weren't as good. On to Mazo where Heritage Days was going on. Ate again, couldn't resist the smell of food cooking outside. It was a hot, humid and windy day. 39 1133
11 June Ten of us left my place for a ride to Lodi. It was warm and humid. After lunch, we went on to Merimac, crossed on the ferry, thought we wanted ice cream, decided we didn't, and went right back again. Then headed to Prairie Du Sac and to Roxbury, where we stopped at the Dorf House for a brief break and back to Middleton. Everyone was very encouraging about my ride in the Rockies, and Jerry, who has ridden the Rockies and the Alps, helped relieve some of my anxieties. I'm going to miss them next week, they will be on their Bayfield ride and I'll probnbly wish more than once that I was there too. 61 1194
