by Joseph King and Scott Rose

Barbara Lukes

DateDescriptionMiles Total
28 March In a cold drizzle I biked over to Jim and Angie's to show them my new bike. 5 5
30 March First group training ride. Were we lucky. Sunny morning, temperature around 50. By afternoon it was cold and snowing. My lack of miles before this first ride showed. I have to train to train with the group. 22 27
1 April Biked to Waunakee for breakfast. It was cold and windy. The pain was awful when my fingers started thawing in Brian's Diner, and I was wearing full-finger gloves. Better going home. 18 45
6 April Out to Kingsley Cemetery and back. 12 57
7 April My friends though we were nuts riding in winds 40-50 mph with gusts up to 60, but I found that after awhile you do sort of aclimate yourself to it. However when Jack decided to cut the ride short, I was right behind him. There was a pretty bad stretch where we couldn't stay on our bikes, had to walk a bit, got an offer for a ride, which Jack turned down. But by turning back and retracing our steps, we did okay. 22 79
8 April A fun ride to Mt. Horeb with Jim and Angie. Stopped at the Riley Tavern for what was supposed to be a short break, but the place was loaded with cyclists and we were really having a good time. Almost succombed to the pancakes, but we do have some discipline and finally got ourselves out of there. Had a nice lunch at Schuberts and enjoyed a tailwind on the way back. 36 115
9 April Out High Road and back Low. Only 8 miles but it was cold and drizzley and I didn't feel like going any further. 8 123
13 April Elaine wanted to take one more test ride on my Cannondale before she made the final decision to buy it. So she and I rode to Waunakee for supper, met Betta and all rode back together. 19 142
14 April A very enjoyable ride to New Glarus with the group. Lunch at the Glarner Stub where I think I left my arm warmers. Have to call them today. The hills were fun, some nice slingshots. Felt good when I got home, so good in fact,I immediately started cleaning the garage. 36 178
16 April A nice morning ride to Waunakee. Didn't know there was a wind til I headed home. 21 199
18 April With Jim and Angie, rode through Shorewood to have supper at Delatalia. 11 210
19 April Rode to Trek to buy a bag. 8 218
20 April Out towards Waunakee and back. 14 232
21 April The ride to Lodi was so much fun! Tailwinds helped me average over 16 mph. Of course we paid the price for that. To say it nicely, going home was not pleasant. And then, about a mile from the park, my chain jammed up. Without a chain tool, we couldn't fix it. A nice man, who was also a bike mechanic at Willy St., stopped and tried to help, but without that tool he couldn't do anything either. So Joe rode to the park and came back to get me in his car. I went directly to Trek, the mechanic put my bike up on the stand and with his little finger put the chain back on. He said it must have worked itself out. He also said my new bike needs a tuneup, so it's going over to Dan's tonight (Monday). 42 274
22 April Breakfast ride to Waunakee. 17 291
24 April Nice evening ride toward Waunakee. 14 305
25 April Rode with Jim and Angie to Waunakee for a Big Mike's sub. 17 322
26 April Met Betta at Vosen and Schneider and rode in to Cross Plains to get something to eat. 19 341
28 April I wish I could be as enthusiasic as the others about this ride. We biked from Sauk City to North Freedom and I was never so tired after a ride, as I was after this one, and that includes RTR last year. I was not prepared for one long hill after the other. But the ride was pretty and I hadn't been to North Freedom in years. 56 397
29 April What should have been a very easy ride was not because of the wind. Always the wind. I rode with my other bike group on their first training ride before their trip in Michigan. 11 of us left John's place in Sun Prairie and rode to Columbus. Along the way, we stopped in Danville to look at an old mill and dam. Didn't stay long, got yelled at for trespassing. Just down the road was Astico where we had a nice lunch on the deck of Shamrock Gardens. When we got back John's wife had ice tea and brownies waiting for us. It was nice to connect with the group again. I like their pace. 45 442
3 May Out to Woodland. Got back just before the rain. 12 454
5 May Another training ride cut short. This time it was cold and raining. We met in Mt. Horeb for a ride to New Glarus. But after about 10 miles it was just too miserable so we turned back. Had a nice lunch at the cafe down the street from Schubert's and went home. 20 474
6 May Despite threatening skies, 11 showed up for the second training ride of the Wright's riders, my other bike group. After coffee and Kringle, we left my place for Sauk City. Just a few sprinkles, comfortable temperatures in the 60's, and a nice tailwind, made for an easy ride there. One of the best lunches you can get is at the Lystra. Most of us had homemade potato soup with our sandwiches,which are made on homemade bread. And the pies. The bumbleberry pie that I had yesterday was good, but the coconut cream I had today was outstanding! The ride back was a little more work. Mostly head winds, but not bad. All in all, a good ride. 49 523
9 May Rode with Angie to Waunakee for a Big Mike's sub. 18 541
13 May Jim, Angie and I drove to Rubicon to do the Holy Hill ride. I love that ride! We started out in Erin Township and biked on roads with names like Druid Lake, Donegal, Shannon, Cavaganauh, St. Augustine. I thought I was riding in Ireland and it was just as beautiful. And always, we could see Holy Hill in the distance. We got on the Kettle Moraine scenic route and rode through Pike Lake State Park. The woods was loaded with Trillium. Lunch was at the Downslope Pub, which in the past had always been good, but this time a disappointment. We had hamburgers and they were a little strange. The texture was odd and they had a different taste. We wondered if we were eating llama burgers since there was a llama farm across the road. We went on to Holy Hill to have our desert. Next time we'll eat our lunch there, the food looked much better. When we arrived, there was a monk blessing cars in the parking lot. Angie thought I should have my bike blessed so if I crashed on RTR I would be assured of a place up above. Going back we missed a turn, and rather than backtrack, because it was getting late, we opted to take the most direct route to the car which was by way of very boring county K and county P. We found a good Mexican restaurant in Watertown for supper. Because we're mothers, Angie and I got a free desert. We decided it was a better Mother's Day than if we had spent it with our kids. Got home at 9, a long day considering we left at 8AM, but a lot of fun. 48 589
15 May Out to highway 19. I really thought it was a side wind heading out but when I turned around to come back realized that the good time I was making going out was mostly due to the tail wind I didn't know I had. 14 603
18 May A different way to Cross Plains. Out Woodland, Springfield-Lodi Road, V, K, KP, Schuman, Otto Kerl and P. Jack and I left my place about 3. It was warm and humid and for awhile I didn't think I was going to make it. I was really going through the water and starting to think I better start rationing it. But fortunately a cloud cover happened and helped things a lot. Ate pizza at Maria's and went back by way of Airport Road, which was a nice change from my usual Schneider Road. 37 640
19 May A beautiful ride to New Glarus. I missed a turn at Jimtown. Jack found me at 69 and Fritz. Darn. I could have taken 69 the 4 miles into New Glarus and waited for the group. But instead I had to do 24 more miles of hills before I could eat lunch.I was glad that it was only 18 miles back to Dan's. 62 702
20 May 10 of us met at Olbrich for the Sytenda Mai ride. It was the usual route through Deerfield, where we stopped for a short break, Lake Ripley to Cambridge, my very favorite part of the ride, lunch at the bakery in Cambridge, on to Stoughton, but first a stop in Utica at the general store to say hello to Barney, the owner. Watched a little of the parade and then went over to the creamery for ice cream. A nice tailwind back to Madison. As always, an enjoyable ride. 64 766
26 May Pat, Joe and I split from Dan, Dennis and Jack on this training ride to Mineral Point. We got on the Military Ridge Trail and rode it to Barneveld and continued on to about 17 miles from Mineral Point. Met the others at the brew pub where we had an excellent lunch. We all rode back to Jack's together. Ran into intermittent rain which made things cold and miserable for awhile but then the sun would come out. We were lucky to get this ride in considering the forecast was for a rainy Saturday. 77 843
27 May Met John, Norm, Jim and Angie in McFarland for a ride to Edgerton. A nice ride on new roads. Lunch was at Ray's Family Restaurant in Edgerton. I had an excellent Taco Salad which was big enough to feed the whole table. The ride back was hard. It was headwind almost all the way, and I was really dragging. Practically collapsed when I got home, and wondered why I can't ride 2 days in a row without becoming so tired. 49 892
28 May Jack designed a very nice route for us leaving from his place to Mt. Horeb. I felt really good. There were some good challenging hills that didn't seem particulary hard. I will never like Blue Mounds Trail though, for a lot of reasons. Sitting outside on flag-lined Main Street in Mount Horeb having lunch was great. Couldn't think of a better way to spend Memorial Day. It was a grand ride Jack! 51 943
30 May Rode to Verona with Jim and Angie for supper. Ate at the truck stop. Had a nice Taco Salad, but no time for dessert. Darn it. 25 968
7 June Rode with Jim and Angie to Cross Plains for supper. Had pizza at Maria's. Back in Middleton we stopped at Culvers for custard. That's where we made our mistake, at least that's what Angie said later. I didn't bother to clip in since I was so close to home, so we crossed the Beltline, and my right foot then proceeded to slip off the pedal and into my front wheel, and I flipped over the handlebars. Fortunately, I ended up with only bruises and sore muscles. 23 991
9 June My bike is up and running again after the 4 broken spokes, broken bar end and rear view mirror from the accident. I didn't do the last rtr training ride today, but instead tested things out by taking a short ride to Magic Mill to buy snacks for the trip. 10 1001
10 June Picked up Angie, met Jack and Betta in Cross Plains and rode to Black Earth for breakfast. Last ride before the rtr. 32 1033
