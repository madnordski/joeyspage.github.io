Bicycling Community Page Training Log, 2001
by Joseph King and Scott Rose

17 March Cold, sunny, and calm. Started at Riley and rode to Klevenville. I took the dead end road out of Klevenville to the east, up the ridge. A nice, long climb up the ridge. I didn't go all the way to the end, just to the top. The only hazzard is two dogs on the last curve near the bottom. 9 9
20 March Met Joe in Paoli for a relatively flat 10 miles. 20 29
30 March My one day off between jobs. I road the route of the first training ride backwards, with a detour into Verona to spend some of my going away gift, a gift certificate at the bike shop. It was cold and cloudy, but calm and actually comfortable. I saw turkeys, got a glimpse of two sandhill cranes in the wetland on Fritz north of A, and a closeup of a turkey vulture. I took a break at the school forest and read the sign and map. The elevation difference is 120 feet on Fritz between the highest and lowest points along the forest. It references an elevation marker on the HWY G overpass over US 18-151. It is 1031 feet. I spent half of my gift certificate on a TOPEAK ALIEN (multi-tool) and a larger, expandable under the seat bag. 29 58
31 March First group ride for the RTR team. Seven of 10 showed up, with four new bikes to ogle. Surprisingly sunny morning with the temparature in the mid 50s, with moderate southerly wind. We started south, after climbing up to Dairy Ridge, taking Sugar River Road from G to Riverside, over to Fritz, South to Wittwer, west to U, picking up the tail wind for the ride north on 96, G, and J, finishing along Dairy Ridge, White Crossing, and Gust. The front came through about 4 hours after we finished, leaving enough snow to cover the ground and not melt until late morning. 22 80
3 April My first commute home. There was a cold NE wind. Fortunately, home is SW of work. The underpass off Grand Canyon still has a small glacier in the middle. The Elver Park parking lot resurfacing doesn't extend to the bike path, so both park paths were the highlight of the ride home. Midtown is damaged and overtaxed with all the condo, home, and golf course construction. The western two miles of it are still very nice. 12 92
4 April Temparature in the low 50s and a cold Easterly Breeze for the first WNBR of the year. It was the traditional kickoff ride from Paoli. 19 111
7 April The route was about as good as we could have given the strong winds. The first 40% was straight into the wind, along Sugar River Rd, Fritz Rd, ending with the climb up peerless. There was some shelter by the hills and school forest. The crosswind on Ridge was brutal. There isn't much of a ditch along most of it, at least where I got blown into it. We cut the ridge ride short and got blown back on U, G, and J. By the time we got up to Dairy Ridge, the wind had shifted to the SW, so we had a nice tail wind instead of a cross wind. The ride back seemed to take no time at all. 27 138
8 April Fresh breeze from the west today, so riding was very pleasant. Headed over to climb Observatory via Riley and Pine Bluff. Saw Evan and Adam before climbing Barlow. With the rest before the climb, I was able to handle the slopes with only couple of traverses. Felt much better than yesterday. 20 158
10 April Rode home from work with a tail wind again. It is only 11 miles if I take the most direct route. 11 169
14 April Rode to Paoli to join the group. No clouds and a tail wind for most of the morning ride to New Glarus. The center of New Glarus is having some utility work done, so only bikes could get through on the main street. The ride leader didn't communicate the route back well enough, so there was a split in the field at one point, with half climbing the larger hill and having more time on the state highway, and half taking the more relaxed route and admiring the young calves. A great day to be on a bike. 44 213
15 April Conditions were cloudy with a cold steady breeze out of the SW. The air was unstable, with occasional very light rain spitting down. Like last Sunday, I rode by Riley and Pine Bluff to climb over Observatory and up Barlow. Barlow was more difficult today. I then went down Barlow, up Garfoot to J, back to P, then took Klevenville-Riley east. Bonnie had run with her friend Deb, and was still talking to her when I rode by. Since my toes were very cold, I stopped in to get a ride home. I also got to see 8 month old Harley, a Black and Tan cocker spaniel in need of a home. If we didn't aleady have three, Harley would be here in short order. 22 235
18 April My SPD-R pedals came today. I tested them with a ride S to Riverview on Sugar River Road, returning on Springrose. The pedals are a big improvement over the SPDs. Easier to get in and out of, with a bigger platform, and workable pontoons. 11 246
19 April Rode to and from work today. Went in on PD and Raymond before cutting over to MidTown. It is just a little more than 10 miles that way, and less hilly than going to Midtown on Timberlane. I came home via Old Sauk to Pioneer. Strong breeze from the south. I didn't have to backtrack at all on the twisty route from Rosa Rd to Yellowstone. 24 270
21 April Rode to Festge Park from Klevenville to meet the group. Strong SSW wind in the morning with lots of scud clouds. Our route took us first North to Crystal Lake, then East to Lodi. We were blown most of the way. After a fine lunch, with spring-fevered ducks for entertainment, we took the shortest way back. The wind had shifted to the West, so we did get some breaks from slogging into it. It wasn't as strong as two weeks ago, but I was getting blown around a little. 59 329
22 April Went out this morning for a 'recovery' ride. Headed S to A via Sugar River and Fritz, returning on Springrose. After the school forest, where a dozen men with one wheel barrow were moving a pile of gravel onto trails, it got darker, and started showering when I was half way across A. There were several short light showers, but no standing water until I got back to G. I got home just as I was starting to feel chilled. There hasn't been any rain since I finished today. 19 348
24 April Rode home from work on a more efficient route than lsat time. It was a pleasant ride. 13 361
25 April Rode to Riley to join the WNR. I was a few minutes late, but didn't see any member of the RTR team. I mostly followed the route, but went up Offendahl, stopped to talk to my vet, then down Springdale Center to rejoin the route on Messerschmidt. I added the hill around Arbor Trace to see what huge houses have gone in since last June. It was a pleasantly cool, very clear evening. 18 379
26 April Beautiful day to commute. The ride in was wind assisted, and the Old Sauk part of the rode wind hindered. It started to feel like summer. 24 403
28 April Perfect temporature, perfect quiet country roads, load training climbs, and a wild section on a one lane,paved twisting road. What more can I ask for? 56 459
1 May I hope I am getting all my flats out of the way this week. Sunday I noticed the Dawes had a flat. I rode to work following the showers, and went flat just after Elver Park. A razor sharp small white stone was imbedded in the tire. Otherwise it was a pleasant ride. I rode the rest of the way on the soft replacement tire. Bonnie dropped off a spare and my old floor pump. Mike brought his in after lunch just in case Bonnie didn't make it. I took it easy on the ride home against the strong SW wind. 24 483
2 May Despite the occasional thunder and dark clouds to the west, we started out with a southerly breeze on the Festge Park WNR. The wind soon dropped. We were in a couple of brief showers on the western edge of the ride, but the storms slid NW of us. It was the pleasantest weather for this ride in several years, almost perfect, in fact. 22 505
5 May It wasn't raining when I started for Mt Horeb, but by the time I started climbing the ridge it was sprinkling, but the time I got to Grundahl park, it the pavement was wet with puddles. Fortunately, after walking the dogs at 8:00 Am I decided to add shoe covers, leg warmers, ear band, and a vest to the day's attire. A strong easterly breeze blew me into Mt Horeb, slightly overheated. I quickly cooled off while waiting for the 9:30 start. As we headed up and down the ridges, it kept getting wetter and darker. After 9 miles we decided to head back. Training is going to well this year to risk overdoing on such a damp cold day. Lunch at the Blue Sky Cafe was excellant. My ride home was comfortable considering the conditions. 35 540
7 May Bonnie dropped me at work with the Waterford. It was a pleasant, cool ride home. I've altered my route to cross Gammon at Colony, but it can take a long time to cross there. It is a quiter section than getting on Old Sauk earlier. 13 553
8 May Nice ride to work with a tail wind, but a stronger head wind for the westerly portion of the ride home. No clouds and pleasantly warm. 24 577
9 May Did the short WNR Oregon loop with the Blanchards and Dennis. A touch cool, but otherwise a nice night to ride. This is the first time I did the short loop. It was nice to finish before dark. 22 599
12 May Started at Governor Dodge, meandered on quite country roads to Avoca for lunch at the Country Inn, came back on even better roads. We deviated early and often from the planned route. 46 645
13 May Beautiful morning to be out. Went down White Xing, Timberlane, J to west, Valley Spring, Klevenville-Riley, P, PD, and Gust. Would have liked to do more, but to many other things are calling today. 15 660
15 May A nice day to commute. I came home the short way. Traffic on Raymond wasn't as bad as I expected. 20 680
16 May WNR out of Bringham Co Park. We did the medium route, forgoing the short loop with another climb. Beautiful night except for the mosquitos. 27 707
18 May 10 miles to work in AM, 10 miles home in PM. A little tail wind to push me home. 20 727
19 May A beatiful, sunny day with quite scenic roads for our meander to New Glarus. Lee Valley and Hay Hollow quiet and closed in. This was the first time I had been on York Center and Badger roads. They stay on the valley floor before gently climbing to A. 44 miles is a long way to lunch, but we survived. Only 18 miles after lunch. I feel suitably drained the day after. 62 789
22 May Nice morning ride to work. Ended up with the car during the day, so only one trip to contribute to Bike to Work week. 10 799
23 May Left work with impeccable timing, before I got to Elver park it was raining. I stopped in a shelter to put on the wind pants and booties. The rain stopped by the time I got to PD. 10 809
25 May Rode to work in a light fog (literally as well as figuratively). It burned off by the time I got to Raymond Road. Left work with even better timing. It started raining when I crossed Whitney, before I got to Odana it was hailing, and rivers were coming out of the parking lots. I stopped in one of the gazebos at Odana and Science Drive to put on the booties and wind pants. By the time I got to Elver the rain had stopped, and the shower hadn't hit Midtown at all. The sun even came out briefly, and a turkey hen ran beside me on Midtown. When I had White Crossing in sight, tho, another heavy shower with hail started. Oh well. At least I was protected and not chilled. 20 829
26 May To Mineral Point from Jack' house. Overheated in the early morning sun on the way up Ryan road. Then the clouds rolled as we went through Blue Mounds before turning south. Pretty nice riding through the small valleys (Mounds view Road?) to F, H, Prairie, Clay Hill, and W before taking 39 in to Mineral Point. The sky was threatening on the way in, and I got a little chilled. Lunch was very good at the Brew Pub. There was a brief shower while we ate. We started when it stopped, but ended up in a light rain, with a brief thunderstorm all the way back to where we cross 151 on Y. The sky started to brighten, and before we got to H, the sun was out warmining and drying us. I thought we would get drenched again, the sky was really black when we started on Knight Hollow, but it passed south of us, and we had a pleasant ride in. 79 908
28 May Headed south on Sugar River Road to Riverside, took Spring Rose down to G, climbed the wall on Kolath, took G to Messerschmidt to Sprindale Center, climbed to Offendahl, down to Town Hall, up to Ridgview, over to P, down to Klevenville-Riley, J to Paulson, Timberlane to WhiteCrossing to Home. Lots of climbing in only 24 miles. 24 932
6 June Back in the saddle after a 9 day layoff due to travel and my lack of desire to ride in cool damp conditions. Got the bike back after a tune up and rode down to Riverside Rd. Still damp and cool, but I feel better for riding. 10 942
8 June Finally commuting weather again. Heavy fog in morning kept obscuring my glasses. Was really strong in PM, made the ride in record time, under 39 minutes elapsed, with a very slight head wind. 20 962
9 June Rode to Paoli to meet the group. One to New Glarus via Observatory, A, Fritz, 92, Argue, Airport, and Valley View. Had a fine picnic lunch out of the bakery. Returned via O, Husted, Ness, JG, Britt Valley, and G. Ness is a nice steady climb without any grades beyond Rockies category. A perfect summer day to ride, finally. 50 1012
10 June Started at 7:30. Cool, mostly cloudy, and humid. Decided to hit roads I haven't ridden on this year. So I rode to XPlains via Timberlane, Birch, Stagecoach and P. Took KP to Garfoot to Braun. I had forgotten how steep the final section of the climb was. Then Union Valley to 78 to Vermont Church to JJ to J. That concluded most of the climbing for the day. Took Boehn to JG to Mt Horeb, then Ridge to P to PD to Gust and home. There were a few rain drops in the last two miles. The wildlife count was one deer, one turkey, one racing ground hog, and a red winged black bird that thought I was a threat and hit me in the helmet. Was accompanied up the final climb by Stewart Park by Nolan, a transplanted southerner who was envious of my RTR jersey. He has done some tough tour riding in North Carolina. 37 1049
11 June Last ride be RTR, a good day to commute. Morning had no wind with temp about 60. Averaged 15 mph. Afternoon was hot, about 88, with a stiff ssw breeze for my wsw ride. Still managed 14.8. 20 1069

Total Miles: 1069
