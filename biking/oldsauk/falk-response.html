Dear Mr. Rider,

Thanks very much for contacting me to express your opposition to expanding Old Sauk Road into four lanes out to Pleasant View Road. I agree with you, and on October 27, 1998 sent the enclosed letter to the members of the City of Madison Pedestrian/Bicycle/Motor Vehicle Commission urging that they consider improvements to Old Sauk Road, similar to the ones made to Seminole Highway, without expanding the road to four lanes.

Old Sauk road is the only remaining major two-lane road connecting the City of Madison to rural Dane County west of the city. It provides a safe and desirable corridor for bicyclists to commute to work or ride into our beautiful countryside.

You may also be interested in two other recent bicycle-related improvements. First, I increased bicycle path funding in the 1999 Dane County budget by $15,000. Second, I have asked the County Highway and Transportation commissioner and County Department of Planning and Development staff to develop standards and a procedure for establishing a "rural scenic byway" program as recommended in 'Design Dane!' One of the purposes of this program would be to help preserve certain roads as scenic corridors and safe bike routes.

Thanks for writing. Please let me know what else I can be doing to help preserve biking opportunities!

Sincerely,

(signed)

Kathleen M. Faulk
Dane County Executive
