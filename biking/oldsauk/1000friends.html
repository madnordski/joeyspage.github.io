Letter from 1000 Friends

November 20, 1998

Mayor Susan Bauman
Office of the Mayor
210 ML King Blvd., Rm 403
Madison, WI 53709

Dear Mayor Bauman:

I understand that Madison's Transportation Department is proposing the expansion of Old Sauk Road into a four-lane highway. 1000 Friends of Wisconsin opposes this expansion.

As you know, 1000 Friends is dedicated to preserving and creating compact, livable communities surrounded by healthy rural and natural areas. To turn Old Sauk into a four lane road would promote low-density sprawl well beyond Madison's borders, blurring the distinction between urban and rural. Ultimately, the quality of life in both places would be reduced.

Madison's Peripheral Development Plan envisions development along Old Sauk to be strictly residential with some retail to service just the surrounding residents. A four-lane highway is not consistent with a livable community. Our fear is that Old Sauk-Pleasantview Road and Old Sauk-Timber Lane will be used by drivers as bypasses of the problems at the Mineral Point-Beltline interchange. It would be far better to fix the problems at that interchange rather than create still further problems along Old Sauk and points west. Madison does not need another West Beltline.

Old Sauk Road is the only remaining rural connection from central Madison to the Driftless Area. For cyclists, the hills of the Driftless Area are prime cycling territory. Since cycling is an important component of livable communities, I urge the City of Madison to preserve this key connector to some of the best cycling in the world. Two lanes with bike lanes is the best plan for this route.

These fingers of the country reaching into the city make the city a better place to live. The city should preserve at least one rural route out of town in each direction. Every other route west has been turned into a four lane highway.

Thank you for considering this request.

Sincerely,

Dave Cieslewicz
Executive Director

cc: Ald. Sue Hamblin
