The following letter was kindly submitted by Mike Barrett.

October 27, 1998

Members, City of Madison Pedestrian/Bicycle/Motor Vehicle Commission
Madison Municipal Building, Room 100
215 Martin Luther King, Jr. Boulevard
Madison, WI 53709

Dear Commission Members:

I understand that the Madison Pedestrian/Bicycle/Motor Vehicle Commission will consider a proposal to expand Old Sauk Road to four lanes out to Pleasant View Road. I have been contacted by constituents who have expressed opposition to this expansion because of, among other things, the negative effect it would have on bicycling.

I have been told that Old Sauk Road is the only remaining major two-lane road connecting the City of Madison to rural Dane County west of the city. It provides a safe and desirable corridor for bicyclists to commute to work or ride into the beautiful countryside surrounding the City, thereby contributing to the high quality of life enjoyed by Madison residents.

I have also been informed that improvements were made to Seminole Highway to accommodate high volume traffic without turning it into a four-lane, high-speed traffic corridor. I respectfully urge the Commission to consider improvements to Old Sauk Road, similar to Seminole Highway, so that it can continue to serve as a hospitable corridor between Madison and rural Dane County.

Thank you very much in advance for your consideration.

Sincerely,

Kathleen M. Falk
Dane County Executive
