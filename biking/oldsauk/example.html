Example Letter to the Editor

Dear Editor,

For a pleasant drive in the country or a bike ride to Black Earth, Old Sauk Road is the best route from Madison. Unlike U.S. 14 to the north and Mineral Point Road to the south, a leisurely pace is welcome on Old Sauk Road.

Unfortunately, the City of Madison has plans to turn Old Sauk Road into a road similar to Mineral Point Road. The plan calls for Old Sauk to be expanded to a divided four lane road west of Junction Road to Prairie Smoke Road.

Planned developments that would be served by the new road are primarily residential. Unfortunately, the plan to expand Old Sauk Road to four lanes seems to be designed to give it a high rating for federal funding without consideration for the people who will live and work nearby, not to mention a greater community that has enjoyed unchanging traffic levels on the more eastern sections of Old Sauk Road for several years. Once this project is completed and congestion on Old Sauk heading downtown increases, Old Sauk east of Gammon and Old Middleton Road to University will be under pressure for expansion. Additionally, plans to align Pleasant View with County M (making a connection to County PD) figure to make Old Sauk a primary connection to this nascent outer beltline.

In response to requests that Old Sauk Road be improved along the lines of Seminole Highway, Mayor Bauman told residents that traffic levels on Old Sauk Road don't compare to those on Seminole Highway according to city staff. This is true, but not in the way implied by her letter. The part of Old Sauk Road planned for expansion to four lanes currently carries about half the traffic that Seminole Highway does near the beltline, according to city records. Seminole Highway along with Fish Hatchery Road serves Dane County's second largest city and is a two lane road that retains a pleasant and rural character.

Let's hope that Mayor Bauman and Alderperson Hamblin don't let the lure of federal funding determine our development plans and strategies. Not every road needs to be Mineral Point Road.

Sincerely,

Joseph J. King, Ph.D.
Bicycle Transportation Alliance of Dane County
