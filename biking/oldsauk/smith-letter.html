Dear Mayor Bauman,

I am writing to you today concerning the proposed widening of Old Sauk Road from the beltline to Prairie Smoke Road. I live in the Estates of Sauk Creek, close to the area under discussion. In the summer, I ride my bike out Old Sauk Road at least five times per week - I feel fortunate to have western Dane County so accessible for biking. I am concerned that the four-lane road out to Prairie Smoke is only the beginning of making Old Sauk Road inaccessible for biking. Currently it is the most used and safest route out to the countryside from West Madison and Middleton.

I can certainly understand that with the addition of Target and other stores that make up the Prairie shopping center, that traffic is particularly heavy on Mineral Point and the widening of Old Sauk Road is seen as an additional outlet for that traffic. Mineral Point Road already has sufficient right of way for it to be made into a four lane road and remain the main automotive artery to western Dane County. Additionally an interchange at High Point Road and the Beltline as well as continuation of Watts Road to from County Highway M to High Point Road can do much to fix the Mineral Point Road problem. The new interchange and road extension are much closer to the business and entertainment districts and are therefore much more logical choices for solving traffic problems.

I further suggest that Old Sauk Road come under the same consideration as Seminole Highway where the two lane road was improved and bike lanes were added. Old Sauk Road has a very similar feeder demography as Seminole Highway - residential, rather than business, that requires access to Madison.

In closing, I would like to remind you that many of the citizens of Madison and Dane county enjoy biking as recreation. My company, Genetics Computer Group (located in the University Research Park) has hired 10 new people over the last year and it is certainly the case that 80% of our new hires as well as our current staff of 25 bike to work on an occasional or frequent basis. The topic of biking comes up at almost every job interview and we have even taken job candidates out to see the ‘bikable’ countryside.

I therefore implore you and the city of Madison to study the greater affects on the community before moving ahead on the four-lane project for Old Sauk Road and carefully consider the alternatives, HighPoint-Beltline interchange and Watts Road extension for relieving the Mineral Point-Beltline distress.

Sincerely,

 

 

Margaret Smith
