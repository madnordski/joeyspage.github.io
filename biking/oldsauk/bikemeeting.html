Dear Cyclist:

The City of Madison is developing a proposal to expand Old Sauk Road to four lanes west of the Beltline to Prairie Smoke Road. Under this proposal, Pleasant View Road would also be expanded to four lanes where it crosses Old Sauk Road. We think that the existing and planned developments in the area would be best served by making two-lane improvements to Old Sauk Road instead.

City staff, KL Engineering, and the Alderperson of the 9th District (Sue Hamblin) have scheduled an open house for cyclists to provide their input on this proposed expansion because of pressure from cyclists. The meeting information is as follows:

Time: 5-7:00 PM
Date: Wednesday, January 13, 1999
Location: Stark Realty Company, 702 N. High Point Rd.
(Corner of Old Sauk Road and High Point Road)

Quiet rural roads are important to Dane County cyclists. Madison cyclists enjoy some of the best cycling in the nation thanks to wonderful roads and the beautiful Dane County countryside. Especially wonderful is how close some of these rural roads are to nearly every Madison resident. Unfortunately, if we do not act now, one of the most precious rural connections is about to be lost and others will follow.

This plan seems to be designed to increase drive through traffic by diverting Beltline traffic from the Mineral Point and US 14 interchanges to Old Sauk Road.I am sure you know how important a cycling route Old Sauk Road is to destinations such as Cross Plains, Black Earth, and Riley. If this proposal goes through, the driftless area will seem further away from every Madison resident, cyclist or not. As a cyclist, you can understand easier than most what effect funneling more Beltline traffic to Old Sauk will have. The traffic levels will increase on the entire length of Old Sauk Road as motorists discover that Timber Lane connects to Mineral Point Road.

Once lost, these rural routes cannot be replaced. Time is short so please act now by attending this meeting and letting the city know Old Sauk Road should look more like Seminole Highway, not Fish Hatchery Road as has been proposed.

Fellow Cyclists

Jeanne Hoffman
