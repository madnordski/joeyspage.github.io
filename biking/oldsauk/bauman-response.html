December 8, 1998

Dear Friend:

Thank you for writing to share your concerns about the possible expansion of Old Sauk Road west of Excelsior Drive adjacent to Old Sauk Trails Research Park. I share your concern about the possible loss of one of the last escape routes from Madison to the unglaciated area to our west. In discussing this issue with Traffic Engineering, the City Engineer, and others, I am persuaded that the Seminole Highway approach to redevelopment of this road will not work in this location. I say this because there is a fundamental difference between the traffic using Old Sauk Road and Seminole Highway. The properties adjacent to Seminole Highway consist of the Arboretum and very low density single family homes. These are not large traffic generators. The areas immediately adjacent to the proposed Old Sauk Road expansion, however, include an office park which has a very large number of persons working there, and development to the south which is becoming increasingly dense.

Contrary to the assertions of some opponents of the proposal for Old Sauk Road, this is not planned as an alternative route to relieve congestion at Mineral Point Road and the Beltline. This road is being studied for possible reconstruction so as to accommodate the people coming from both the east and the west to gain access to worksites at Old Sauk Trails Park. As such, it si imperative to provide sufficient roadway capacity to allow both straight ahead and turning cars.

I have requested that the City Engineer review the proposal for expansion and to look for a configuration which will have the least impact on the environment of the surrounding area. It is my understanding that the current plans call for a divided roadway with a median strip and two lanes with a bicycle lane in each direction. The inclusion of the median strip already means that the road will be more user friendly than Old Sauk Road immediately to the east of the Beltline. I have specifically asked for the Engineering folks to review streets like Tokay Boulevard which do allow for two lanes of traffic and a median strip. Tokay Boulevard does not have bicycle lanes on both sides but also does not bear the amount of traffic which it is anticipated that Old Sauk Road will be carrying in the near future if, in fact, it is not already carrying.

Some people have written and talked about studies they have seen which indicate that increasing capacity of certain roadways does not improve traffic flow. That may be the case and, in fact, may prove to be the case here. The inclusion of segregated bicycle lanes, turning lanes, and the like will, of necessity, result in improved safety, one of the desired goals of this project.

Modest revisions which improve the plan, make it bicycle friendly, pedestrian friendly, and automobile friendly are being developed. We must realize that maintenance of the current situation does not meet any of these goals and, in fact, is environmentally unsound given the amount of pollution which s generated by exhaust through periods of congestion.

Thank you for writing and expressing your concerns. Please keep watching this project and advise me of your responses, both positive and negative, to any alterative [sic] schemes which may be put forth by Traffic Engineering or the City Engineer.

Sincerely,

Susan J. M. Bauman [signed]
Mayor
