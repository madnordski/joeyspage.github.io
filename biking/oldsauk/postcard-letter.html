Dear Cyclist:

Quiet rural roads are important to Dane County cyclists. Madison cyclists enjoy some of the best cycling in the nation thanks to wonderful roads and the beautiful Dane County countryside. Especially wonderful is how close some of these rural roads are to nearly every Madison resident. Unfortunately, if we do not act now, one of the most precious rural connections is about to be lost and others will follow.

The City of Madison is developing a proposal to expand Old Sauk Road to four lanes west of the Beltline to Prairie Smoke Road. Under this proposal, Pleasant View Road would also be expanded to four lanes where it crosses Old Sauk Road. We think that the existing and planned developments in the area would be best served by making two-lane improvements to Old Sauk Road instead. Old Sauk Road should look more like Seminole Highway, not Fish Hatchery Road as has been proposed.

As you may know, traffic has been increasing steadily on Old Sauk Road for a number of years. Road closures last spring that limited use of Old Sauk Road to local traffic make it unambiguously clear that the increase in traffic is primarily due to drive through traffic. This plan seems to be designed to increase drive through traffic by diverting Beltline traffic from the Mineral Point and US 14 interchanges to Old Sauk Road. Instead of using Old Sauk Road for this purpose, we think improvements to the Beltline interchanges should be considered before all of the rural routes heading out of Madison are lost.

I am sure you know how important a cycling route Old Sauk Road is to destinations such as Cross Plains, Black Earth, and Riley. If this proposal goes through, the driftless area will seem further away from every Madison resident, cyclist or not. As a cyclist, you can understand easier than most what effect funneling more Beltline traffic to Old Sauk will have. The traffic levels will increase on the entire length of Old Sauk Road as motorists discover that Timber Lane connects to Mineral Point Road.

Please address the enclosed postcards to your representatives and include your return address on the front. On the back, please sign the message. Please add postage and mail today.

Once lost, these rural routes cannot be replaced. Time is short so please act now.

 

Joseph J. King, Ph.D.

Fellow Cyclist
