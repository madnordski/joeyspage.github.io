Bicycling Community Page Training Log for 1998

Joe

This will be last weekend of training for me. No wonder I'm depressed.

DateDescriptionMiles
26 March Ride through the arb. 10
28 March Biked from Dan's with Jack and Dan. A nice ride under cloudy skies. 19.5
2 April A ride through the arb on my new bike. It is nice and confortable but the gearing is way too low as expected. 10
4 April We had a great ride under sunny skies. The route was hilly and the head wind was a cool breeze out of the north east. I rode back from Dan's after the ride. My detour to Pasqual's added an additional two miles to the ride. I'm beat. 38.2
7 April I discovered that I could do the arb loop, get lunch at Milan's sandwich shop, and eat in just an hour fifteen. My odometer recorded 10.0 miles from work to Milan's via the arb. Which means that it must be about 9.5 miles when I skip lunch. 10
9 April Another loop through the arb. This time under grey skies and a cold north wind. I've modified the loop so as to get an honest ten miles. I guess I'll subract a mile from today's ride to account for the over estimates entered earlier. 9.1
10 April Skipped out on work and went for sparwl ride through Fitchberg. Coming back to town on Fishhatchery Road was OK until Badger Rd. where the road narrows and a rough RR crossing nearly took me down. 19.8
11 April Jack, Mike, Jeremy and I did the Belville Wednesday Night Ride from Paoli. It was a wonderfully picturesque, windy, and hilly. I backed back to Madison from Paoli for an extra 15 miles and a few more hills than I rcalled. 53.1
15 April It was cold, wet and windy but it was Wednesday and it was a ride. I did the short route because, unlike last Saturday the wind was out of the northeast. The top of Boul hill was particularly beautiful as a break in the clouds lit up the countryside to the east. 17.5
16 April I took the long, cold, and windy way home via lake Mendota. It was nice to see a different lake for a change but when the ride ended I was in some real pain from cold hands. 6
17 April A bright sun shown through nubile leaves along Arboretum drive today. The wind was from the west but it seemed to have been muted by the rapidly greening trees. 12.3
18 April Rode with Jack and Dan from Cross Plains to Roxbury and back. There was a stiff wind from the southwest and a bright sun overhead. We had no trouble locating some big hills. I totally enjoyed the downside. 49
19 April Pat and I did the short WNR from Paoli. It was a beautiful day to be out. We ran into about hundred Bombay riders along the way. A nice short ride felt good after yesterdays. Still, I need to drink more. 12
22 April A wonderful spring evening for a bike ride. Mike and I enjoyed watching the sun set on our ride as we approached Riley. 24
24 April This was a great ride for through the arb. There was a lot going on including a crew meet on lake Wingra with teams from Kansas and Colorado. 10.4
25 April It was a cool and wonderful ride to New Glarius with Jack and Dan. 68
26 April A good commuter bike and a keep-you-dry rain suite really come in handy on cold raining days like today. The fruit trees in the arb are beginning to bloom. 16.3
29 April I was knocked out of a deep daydream by a wall of fragrance near the McKay center in the arb. The lilacs are out!!!! 11.5
3 May After leaving our joint ride I headed west to Black Earth so that I could reach my goal. It was cold but the entire ride was sweet. 80.4
4 May A beautiful day for a picinic with Pat in the arb. 9.5
5 May A vigorous ride through the arb. 12
6 May It was pouring rain when i left Madison. Although it was not raining at the start of the ride, dark skys loomed overhead. The wind was calm and the air was cool and scented with apple blossums. The biking fast and sweet. The rain, most fortunately, held off until we were done. 22.7
8 May Ride to work via Seminole Hwy, Fitchrona Rd., Maple Grove, and Whitney Way. Due to the closure of Nesbit and Fitchrona Rd. (but passable by bike) this was a very pleasant ride (i.e. quiet roads). 15
9 May We had a beautiful sunny but cool day for a long ride. For me it was the first time on many of the road to Blanchardville. There were some real gems. Lunch at the Blanchard was delivered with the utmost slowness but it seemed to have been prepared with care from scratch. From the fries to the pie there was nothing that said mass produced. Pie, you ask? The blackberry pie was well done indeed. Move over Norskie Nook, Wisconsin has a better "best place" for pie! 89.7
13 May I rode from work to Oregon averaging 16mph and then did the Wednesday Night Ride there averaging 11mph. Pat and I have been so busy lately it was pure joy biking and talking with her last night. 45
14 May A fast arb loop with Steve Goldstein. 10
15 May Down toward Pailoi was my route to work this morning. I found that County M is closed just west of Whalen which when combined with the Fitchrona/Nesbit closures is making for some nice quiet biking. It suggests that we should just close all the roads to thru traffic (except bikes). 22
16 May A page out of Dan's book: Old Sauk, Cty P, Cty KP, Garfoot, Cty J, Maurer, Timber, Old Sauk. Every windy going out, very fast coming back. 37.4
17 May Bombay ride to Stougton. Despite a strong wind out of the southwest, I finished the ride with a 16mph average. This mostly because I biked with Evan who with is 14yr old son Andy is in training for ride the rockies. 80
20 May Oregon-Paoli-Verona View Ride: Pat and I started out together heading toward Paoli. Then I continued on a route to work that included the entire length of Purcell Rd. and views of Oregon and Verona. Road construction on Rimrock Rd. that helped make the ride to work from the south nice has now made it extremely unpleasant. Today they started putting down the asphalt. I won't make the Wednesday Night Ride tonight because of a work related social. 30.9
23 May The air was so clear it seemed to magnify the rolling country side of Iowa County. Despite a cool breeze that blew us west, we didn't think we could make it to Plain Jane's Cafe before she closed. Lunch in Spring Green was probably more heathful anyway. 75
25 May Pat and I did a Riley ride that included a stop at Kit's Corner for lunch. It was another absolutely beautiful Wisconsin day. Despite a few hills, Pat averaged 11.5mph which is good for her longest ride of the year. 35
27 May Biked hard from work to Salmo Pond averaging 18mph. Then it was another 20 miles of easy biking with Pat. This is one of the most beautiful Wednesday Night Ride. 40
30 May See Pie Ride from Cross Plains to Plain. 97
1 June Biked to Fitchburg for a business meeting with Berbee. There was a tough head wind out of the south. The ride back was nice. 15
3 June Another one of those absolutely beautiful Wednesday Night Rides. I have to thank Pat for pushing me out the door to do this ride. 27

Total Miles: 1140.3
