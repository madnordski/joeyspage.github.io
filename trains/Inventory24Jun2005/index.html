Shots of Structures

These photos are of various structures in different states of completeness. I was just beginning the scenery when it was decided to take down the trains to make way for other things. Some of the builds were purchased, built from kit, or scratch built.

Train Inventory & Photos, June 2005

Post-war Vintage
Lionel 671
Pennsylvania Steam Turbine		 Purchased in Madison in 1988.  Runs better forward than in reverse.  The tender whistle needs a good clean and lube.  It tends to come off the tracks on certain switches.  Vintage, late 40's or early 50's.
Lionel 2055
Steam Engine		 Gift from OLIS in 1986.  This steamer is not as powerful as the turbine, L671.  The tender whistle needs a good clean and lube.  Vintage 1953.
Lionel 2333
New York Central F3 Diesel		 Gift from Uncle Roy when I was young (in the 1960's).  The bodies of both the engine and dummy were abused when I was a kid.  The wiring has been replaced.  The E unit doesn't always work.  The electrol couplers have been completely disabled so that they stay closed over a variety of switches.  Recently, I had to drill out the battery door screw and replace the old thumb screw with a self tapping philips head.  The dummy body has a very large crack along the top.  Also, the front NYC decal has peeled off of both bodies.  It might be possible to improve the looks of both bodies with some well matched gray paint.  Vintage, late 1940's or 1950's.
Lionel 2348
Minneapolis & St. Louis GP-9 Diesel		 Runs well. Like the others of this vintage it has magnatraction.  I think I purchased this in the late 1980's but I don't recall exactly.  Vintage, 1958.
Lionel 210
Texas Special Alco FA-2 Diesel		 Runs but not well in both directions.  Like the others of this vintage it has magnatraction but no horn.  This is one of several engines that my father discovered at a garage sale. Vintage, 1958.
Lionel 1110
Scout Steam Locomotive		 Runs but not well in both directions.  Unlike the others of this vintage it does not have has magnatraction which is what places this engine to 1951 or 1952 (even though these were made as early as 1949).  It has a light, two direction E-unit, and no smoke unit.  I'm not sure whether I have the original tender or not as they are all mixed up.  Vintage 1951 or 1952.
Lionel 520
Box Cab Electric,
GE 80 Ton Locomotive		 Given to me by my father.  He found it at a garage sale.  It has only the Lionel name on it but it is a prototype of the GE 80 ton locomotive.  The pantograph is incomplete.  Vintage 1956.
Lionel 1882
The General Steam Locomotive		 Given to me by my father.  The General is a Western and Atlantic steam engine.  This one happens to be an uncatalogued promotional item made in 1960 (orange wheels and cab).  There is no smoke or horn (as best as I can tell).  It runs fine with a two-direction E-unit.  There is apparently a Disney movie called the Great Locomotive Chase that is based on a civil war event the involved the theft of the General by the north.  The General was chased appearently by another engine known as the Texas. Vintage 1960.
Lionel 50
Section Gang Car		 Given to me by my father.  Needs maintenance before it can be run again.  Vintage 1955.
Middle or Unknown Vintage
Lionel 8770
EMD NW-2 Diesel-Electric		 Runs fine.  From my father.  Light, two position E unit, no horn, and no operating couplers.  The drive truck is metal and the other truck is plastic.  Look is similar to a Lionel 600 EMD originally made in 1955.  Likely vintage, 1970's.
Marx 490
Plastic 0-4-0 Steam Engine		 Runs fine.  From my father.  Plastic, has a light (not working), two position E unit, no horn, no smoke, and no operating couplers.  Unknown vintage.
Modern Vintage
Lionel 7420
New York Central GP-7 Diesel		 Runs perfectly.  I purchased this in the 1990's.  Modern (1990's) vintage.
Lionel 8922
New Haven Diesel		 Runs perfectly.  I purchased this in the 1990's with a matching set of passenger cars (see below).  Also, at about the same time a set of New York Central Passenger cars were purchased.  Modern vintage.
MTH
Commodore Vanderbilt		 I purchased this in the 1990's.  It has rail sounds with a really fun passenger station recording.  Unfortunately, it stopped working altogether in 2005.  When power is applied it clangs three times and sits.  It doesn't seem to respond to any commands and it will not move.  I'm guessing the replacing the battery is what is needed.  New Info: When I was putting it away I noticed that the rubber drive tire was broken, exposing the metal wheel underneath.  It could be that is the cause of the problem. I also noticed that there are two replacement rubber tires in the box.  When it did run it ran well, produced lots of good smoke and the digit horn and bell recordings were excellent.  Modern vintage.
Lionel Work Car
Modified		 This simple work car was modified to dust the track.  It has no E-unit.  I use Lionel track cleaning fluid on the dusting cloth.  The roller does not roll but glides over the track.  Turn the roller to use a cleaner part of the cloth.  Be sure to apply cleaning fluid to the part of the dusting cloth that will glide over the track.  I purchased this new in the 1990's.
Lionel 58 PW
Great Northern Railway Rotary Snowplow		 The snow plow has a new electronic three position reversing unit that always starts in neutral.  Cycle the power twice to get it moving.  The little bell on top rocks as if to ring which is very cute.  It has a pullmore motor, headlamp, rotating snow impeller, and a post-war builders plaque.  Purchased in 2000. Produced in 1999.  Catalog number Lionel 6-18446.
Lionel 55
Pennsylvania RR Tie-Jector		 This is an operating car with no reversing unit.  It runs on it's own and spits out RR ties as it goes.  There are two track sidings provided that can be used to throw a lever on this car to turn on and off the tie spitting action.  Very fun.  I purchased this in 1999 or thereabouts.  It was built in 1997.
Operating Accessories
 also: MOVIE Lionelville
Saw Mill		 This is accessory creates the illusion that logs are being cut up into planks of wood. The log dump car, L3351, dumps a load of logs onto the platform. The mill is then activated by a button. The logs are taken into the mill and cut boards appear from the other side. The cut boards are loaded into a slot in the top of the mill and the logs taken in are retrieved by opening a door in that back of the mill. This was purchased in the 1990's and is modern vintage.
 also: MOVIE Lionelville
Pipe Company		 This is accessory creates the illusion that pipes are being loaded into a box car.  The beauty is, any box car will do since the pipes are actually retracted back into the forklift.  The movie says it all.  I purchased this in the 1990's sometime.
 also: MOVIE Lionelville
Ice House		 This is accessory is a modern version of an old post-war favorite.  The refrigerator car is filled with blocks of ice by the man on top of the structure.  The movie says it all.  I purchased this in the 1990's sometime.
Lionel
Lumber Loader (6-12774) & Norev Minature Cyclists		 This is accessory is a manually operated lumber loader. The logs are pushed one at a time from the platform into the house and a lever on the back is depressed to release the log down onto a log dump car. This snap-together kit was fully assembled and then disassembled for repacking into the box pictured. The roof did not come apart and barely fits in the box as-is.

Also pictured are several pro-cyclist minatures. They are metal, painted, made in France and of good quality. Only one has ever been out of the original box. I've got team Telecom (6 cyclists, 4006) as well as riders for Peugeot (4503), Kas (4502), Bic (4505), Margant (4504), Festina (4521) and Molteni (4501).
Lionel 6-12735
Diesel Horn Shed		 This is accessory provides a diesel horn sound. It's handy to use with those locomtives with broken horns or no battery. The sound is not great but is as good or better than most diesel horns included with post-war engines.

Operating Milk Car		 This is one of the funnest items Lionel ever made.  The special track activates the car causing a man to bust through the metal doors and shove a milk can onto the platform.  The cans have little magnets in them so that don't always go flying.  I purchased this in 1989 or thereabouts.  It was produced in 1948 and 1949.

Operating Searchlight Car		 The magnetic track can be used to turn the searchlight on or off.  This version was produced in 1950 or 1951.

Lionel 2460
Bucyrus Erie Class 250 Crane Car		 This is a quality car with a manually operated crane.  I converted an old flat car to serve as the compagion utility car and to keep the crane boom from moving around.  The crane car was urchased in 2000 or the late 90's.  It was built in 1997.
Lionel 16619
Operating Coal Dump Car		 This car uses the magnetic track to dump its load, typically coal.  Purchased in the 1990's, built 1990.
Lionel 16619
West Side Lumber Company Log Dump Car		 This car uses the magnetic track to dump its load, typically logs.  This car was the perfect find for me because I had planned to model the fictional village of Velocipede and was basing it on the actual village of Lombard, IL.  For many years in Lombard a lumber area dominated the track side envirnoment and Lombard is a western suberb of Chicago.  What better place could there be for the West Side Lumber Company?  Purchased in the 1990's, built 1999.
